Tools are not neutral. They install opinions in the hands that use them daily — and those opinions outlast the evaluation period.

I felt this rebuilding the darkheartlabs streaming stack: the chat client that pings on every sub, the editor tuned for snippet completion over long-form refactors, the alert sound mapped to urgency whether or not the moment deserved it. Each choice made reactive work feel easy and contemplative work feel like swimming upstream. The tools were not bad. They were neighbours whose habits I was adopting without noticing.

The mechanism is practice, not preference. A worse tool aligned with your taste produces better work than a better tool that fights it — because you will adapt to the tool long before the tool adapts to you. Keyboard layout, linter defaults, notification cadence: all of it trains muscle memory and attention.

Choose tools the way you choose neighbours. Audit the interrupts before you audit the features. You will adopt their habits whether you mean to or not.

— JV · Dark Heart Labs.
