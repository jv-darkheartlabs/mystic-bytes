Management is a bandwidth problem. The job is not knowing the answers — it is allocating attention across a problem space that always exceeds supply.

I learned this the hard way running a small platform team through a quarter where every stakeholder had a P0. The roadmap did not fail because we lacked skill. It failed because I never named what we were publicly stopping. Unmade decisions leak: the team keeps spending compute on work leadership has silently deprioritized, because silence reads as "maybe still important." Three half-funded initiatives cost more than one finished one.[^1]

New managers optimize for coverage — be helpful, stay informed, keep options open. Experienced managers optimize for throughput — decide, communicate the cut, absorb the discomfort so the team can stop guessing.

Spend bandwidth like it costs real money — because it does, in slipped dates and quiet attrition. Every item you refuse to rank is a tax on everyone downstream.

— JV · Dark Heart Labs.

[^1]: Andy Grove, *High Output Management* (Random House, 1983) — on managerial leverage and the cost of undistributed decisions across an organization.
