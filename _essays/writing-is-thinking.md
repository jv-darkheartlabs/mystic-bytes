Writing is not a way to communicate thinking that already happened elsewhere. For most working professionals, writing is where the thinking actually happens.

The mystic-bytes rewrite pass proved this in the negative: we agreed in a call that essays should be "more mechanism, less metaphor." The first drafts without written criteria drifted back to transmission-log tone inside a week. Once the style guide forced every essay to declare system, issue, and constraint in the brief, the arguments got shorter and the merges got faster. The fuzzy idea in someone's head became specific only when forced through the bottleneck of a sentence someone else could reject.

The mechanism is commitment. Talk feels like progress and produces fewer obligations. Writing produces fewer feelings and more decisions — testable claims, named tradeoffs, diffs that stick.

Write the memo before the meeting. The memo is the work, not its summary.

— JV · Dark Heart Labs.
