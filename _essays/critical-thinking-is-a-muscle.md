Critical thinking is not a temperament — it is a trained capacity that decays when teams optimize for agreement.

I have watched this on the mystic-bytes essay rewrite pass: the easy move is to smooth every claim into consensus language and lose the mechanism on the way. Disagreement aimed at ideas is not toxicity. Reading work you disagree with — on purpose — is the rep, and the discomfort is the load.

Most sprint rituals reward alignment, not examination. Standups surface status. Slack punishes slow replies. The senior engineer who has not defended an unpopular technical read in three months has not been on sabbatical from conflict. The muscle has atrophied, and the team's collective judgement softens with it.

You do not need performative debate. You need a recurring slot where someone brings a contrarian read and the room tries to falsify it — or updates its own model.

Schedule the disagreement before production schedules it for you.

— JV · Dark Heart Labs.
