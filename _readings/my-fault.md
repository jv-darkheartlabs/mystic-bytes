## Hook & thesis
In *My Fault*, Mercedes Ron deftly navigates the treacherous waters of forbidden love, unearthing the complexities of desire that lie beneath societal taboos. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully intertwines passion and peril, crafting a narrative that challenges both the characters and the reader to confront their own moral thresholds.

## The contextual pivot
Situated at the intersection of dark romance and contemporary young adult fiction, *My Fault* resonates within a lineage that includes titans like Anna Todd’s *After* and Colleen Hoover’s *It Ends with Us*. Ron draws upon the allure of step-sibling romance — a provocative trope that flirts with societal norms while inviting a vast audience ranging from teenage readers to adults grappling with the complexities of desire. Ron's work, previously known for its gripping emotional arcs, pushes boundaries here, engaging both niche enthusiasts and general readers alike with a narrative that is both rebellious and relatable.

## Deep-dive critique
At its core, *My Fault* thrives on its structure, employing a dual perspective that immerses readers in the tumultuous emotions of both protagonists. The pacing oscillates between breathless tension and reflective moments, mirroring the characters’ internal struggles and their escalating romance. Ron’s prose pulses with urgency and vulnerability, particularly in dialogue that reveals the characters' raw desires and the societal constraints that threaten to suffocate them. 

However, the novel occasionally stumbles with pacing, particularly in the middle sections, where the emotional weight seems to lag as the protagonists grapple with their decisions. This could risk losing readers who crave a sustained intensity. The thematic exploration of guilt and desire is compelling, yet at times, the characters teeter on the edge of predictability, echoing familiar archetypes without fully subverting them. The ending, while impactful, leaves some threads unresolved, inviting readers to ponder the implications of their choices but also feeling somewhat abrupt in its conclusion, which may alienate those seeking closure.

## Discussion launchpad
The polarizing elements in *My Fault* hinge on the moral implications of its central romance and the choices made by its characters. Does embracing taboo love lead to liberation or entrapment? How do societal norms shape our understanding of desire and morality? These questions linger beneath the surface, inviting rich discussion.

**Questions for the room:**

- What moral dilemmas does the book present, and how do they resonate with modern readers?  
- In what ways does Ron successfully challenge or reinforce traditional romantic tropes?  
- How does the pacing affect your engagement with the characters and their journey?  
- Do you find the ending satisfying, or does it leave too much unresolved?
