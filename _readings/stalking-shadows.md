## Hook & thesis
In *Stalking Shadows*, Lisa J. Morgan masterfully weaves a tapestry of obsession and desire, culminating in a narrative that seduces while it terrifies. **Verdict:** This is a chilling exploration of the blurred lines between love and madness that leaves an indelible mark on the psyche.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned firmly within the stalker-obsession subgenre, Morgan's work resonates with the gothic echoes of authors like Anna Zaires and A. Zavarelli, while also carving its own niche. It deftly bridges the gap between niche dark romance aficionados and a broader audience who crave psychological depth in their love stories. The themes of longing and peril are universal, suggesting that even those unfamiliar with darker tropes will find themselves entranced by Morgan’s compelling prose and layered characters.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Stalking Shadows* employs a tight third-person limited POV that allows readers to inhabit the protagonist's mind while simultaneously fostering a sense of disorientation and danger. This choice enhances the tension, as the reader is privy to the protagonist's troubling thoughts and obsessive tendencies, heightening the emotional stakes. Morgan's pacing is deliberate, unfolding the narrative with a slow burn that mirrors the protagonist's escalating fixation. The dialogue is sharp and often laced with subtext, revealing the characters’ hidden motives and desires, while the world-building evokes a haunting atmosphere that feels both intimate and claustrophobic.

Despite its strengths, the novel occasionally stumbles in its thematic execution. While the exploration of obsession is nuanced, there are moments where the line between romantic longing and unhealthy fixation becomes muddied, risking the romanticization of toxic behaviors. The ending, though impactful, may not resonate with all readers, as it challenges conventional notions of resolution, leaving some threads tantalizingly unresolved.

## Discussion launchpad
Morgan's *Stalking Shadows* elicits a dual response: it draws readers in with its seductive narrative while simultaneously provoking contemplation on the nature of obsession and consent. This juxtaposition could ignite a fervent debate on the ethical implications of such relationships in literature.
