## Hook & thesis
In *Stalking Shadows*, Lisa J. Morgan masterfully delves into the tumultuous interplay of obsession and identity, revealing the chasms of human desire that can both bind and destroy. **Verdict:** This novel operates as a haunting exploration of the psyche, where the boundary between love and madness blurs disturbingly into a chilling portrait of obsession.

## The contextual pivot
As a contribution to the stalker-obsession subgenre, *Stalking Shadows* stands alongside works like *You* by Caroline Kepnes and *The Collector* by John Fowles. Morgan’s narrative offers a visceral examination of the human condition, capturing the invisible audience of readers who are drawn to the darker recesses of interpersonal relationships. It deftly bridges niche tropes of psychological thrillers and romance, inviting general readers into a world where the line between passion and peril is tantalizingly thin.

## Deep-dive critique
Morgan's craft shines through in her meticulous structure, employing a dual perspective that oscillates between the stalker and the stalked. This duality invites readers into the minds of both characters, amplifying the tension that defines their destructive relationship. The pacing is deliberately slow, mirroring the psychological spiral that envelops the protagonist, allowing the reader to digest the emotional nuances of each interaction. For instance, dialogue is laden with subtext, where seemingly innocuous exchanges simmer with underlying threat, and the prose itself often reads like a fever dream — fluid and disorienting, leaving readers questioning reality.

However, the novel occasionally falters in its pacing, particularly in the latter half, where it risks losing momentum as the stakes evolve. The ending, while dramatic, may leave some readers feeling that the resolution is not fully earned, as it relies heavily on a final twist that teeters on the edge of believability. Still, Morgan's exploration of themes like the duality of human nature and the allure of danger is both provocative and unsettling, making a lasting impression long after the final page.

## Discussion launchpad
While *Stalking Shadows* captivates with its intense exploration of obsession, it also polarizes readers regarding its treatment of love and mental instability. The ethical implications of romanticizing such relationships may ignite fervent discussion.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of obsession in the novel romanticize toxic relationships, or does it serve as a cautionary tale?  
- How does the dual perspective shape our sympathy for the characters involved?  
- In what ways does the narrative challenge or reinforce traditional gender roles within the context of obsession?  
- Is the psychological intensity depicted in the novel an accurate reflection of real-life stalking scenarios?  
- How does the ending influence your overall perception of the story's themes?
