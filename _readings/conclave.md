## Hook & thesis
In **Conclave**, Penelope Douglas crafts a dark, atmospheric exploration of obsession and power dynamics within a secret society, framed through the lens of a bullying romance. **Verdict:** This novella masterfully entwines psychological tension with gothic undertones, revealing the insidious nature of desire hidden beneath its seductive surface.

## The contextual pivot
Set within the burgeoning subgenre of bully romance, **Conclave** positions itself amidst Douglas’s established *Devil's Night* series, aimed at readers who revel in the intersection of darkness and desire. This novella deftly bridges niche tropes with broader themes of social hierarchy and emotional turmoil, appealing not just to dedicated fans of the genre but also to readers seeking gothic romance that challenges conventional narratives. The themes of obsession and power are universal, yet the secretive backdrop of elite societies adds a layer of intrigue that enhances its appeal.

## Deep-dive critique
Douglas employs a nonlinear structure that skillfully weaves past and present, creating a complex narrative tapestry that mirrors the protagonist's internal struggles. The shifting perspectives deepen the reader's understanding of the characters’ motivations, particularly in how bullying manifests as both a weapon and a means of connection. For instance, the dialogue crackles with tension, reflecting the characters' emotional barriers while simultaneously revealing their vulnerabilities. This duality is potent; it allows readers to empathize with both the victim and the aggressor, blurring moral lines that are often clearly drawn in conventional narratives.

Moreover, the atmospheric world-building is richly detailed, immersing the reader in a setting that feels both lavish and claustrophobic. The opulence of the secret society contrasts sharply with the underlying violence, enhancing the thematic exploration of desire intertwined with danger. However, while the prose is generally strong, certain moments in the narrative could benefit from more subtlety, occasionally tipping into melodrama that risks overshadowing the nuanced themes at play. The ending, while satisfying, leaves some threads tantalizingly unresolved, inviting readers to contemplate the implications of obsession and power long after the final page.

## Discussion launchpad
The polarities within **Conclave** stem from its provocative themes and complex character dynamics, which can elicit strong reactions from readers. The juxtaposition of desire and violence within the context of a bullying romance raises questions about moral ambiguity and the nature of obsession.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of obsession in the characters’ relationships romanticize unhealthy dynamics, or does it serve as a cautionary tale?  
- How do the themes of secrecy and power within the society reflect real-world issues of privilege and abuse?  
- In what ways does the nonlinear structure enhance or detract from the emotional impact of the story?  
- Can the reader sympathize with the characters’ actions, given their morally ambiguous choices?  
- What role does setting play in shaping the characters’ motivations and desires throughout the novella?
