## Hook & thesis
In Brynne Weaver's *Love & Other Killers*, the collision of affection and annihilation reveals the intoxicating, treacherous dance of love in a world where desire is a deadly game. **Verdict:** This novella masterfully intertwines dark humor and romantic tension, crafting a narrative that is both thrilling and unsettling.

## The contextual pivot
Weaver's contribution to the enemies-to-lovers trope is a bold exploration of the intersections between romance and danger, placing her firmly within the dark romance subgenre. While the novella embraces familiar elements of romantic comedy, it does not shy away from the grotesque, creating a unique blend that positions it alongside authors like Talia Hibbert and Elizabeth O'Roark, yet with a distinctly more perilous edge. The invisible audience here is composed of readers who revel in the darker shades of love—those who appreciate humor laced with menace, and who are drawn to narratives that challenge conventional romantic ideals.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Love & Other Killers* is both tight and expansive, allowing for a brisk pacing that juxtaposes the flirtation of romance with the looming threat of violence. Weaver employs a dual POV that adds layers of complexity to the characters, enabling readers to oscillate between the protagonists' conflicting desires and motivations. This choice not only enhances the tension but also amplifies the stakes, as each character discovers the darker desires that fuel their animosity.  

Weaver's prose is sharp, rich with wit that cuts through the narrative, often revealing the characters’ vulnerabilities amidst their bravado. Dialogue is snappy, transporting readers into a world where humor serves as both a shield and a weapon. However, while the thematic exploration of love as a potentially lethal force is compelling, the novella occasionally falters in its character depth; some motivations seem muddled or underexplored, leaving the reader craving a deeper understanding of the psychological underpinnings driving the characters’ actions. The ending, while exhilarating, risks feeling unearned without sufficient emotional groundwork laid throughout the story, leaving a lingering desire for resolution beyond the physical.

## Discussion launchpad
*Love & Other Killers* is poised to spark intriguing debates about the nature of love and the boundaries between affection and aggression. The juxtaposition of romance and violence invites rich discussions on the morality of desire and the implications of viewing love through a dark lens. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the blending of humor with dark themes enhance or detract from the romantic experience?  
- How do the protagonists’ flawed motivations shape our understanding of love in this narrative?  
- In what ways does this novella challenge or reaffirm the traditional enemies-to-lovers trope?  
- Are we to sympathize with characters who find love in the midst of chaos, or does this diminish the seriousness of their actions?  
- How does the novella's pacing affect our engagement with the moral complexities presented?
