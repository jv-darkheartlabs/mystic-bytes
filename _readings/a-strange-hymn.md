## Hook & thesis
In Laura Thalassa's **A Strange Hymn**, the narrative unfolds like a haunting melody, intertwining themes of desire and power within a dark, fantastical realm. **Verdict:** Thalassa's deft handling of gothic romance elements elevates the book from mere genre fare to a compelling exploration of the human psyche caught in the throes of love and betrayal.

## The contextual pivot
This sequel in the Bargainer Series navigates the murky waters of fantasy-romance, a genre often overshadowed by its more mainstream counterparts. Thalassa's work draws on a blend of fae mythology and dark magic, positioning itself alongside authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout, yet it carves its own niche by delving into the more visceral aspects of desire and emotional conflict. The book caters to an audience that revels in the tension of dark romance while maintaining enough accessibility for readers new to the genre, creating a bridge that invites exploration beyond niche tropes.

## Deep-dive critique
Thalassa's prose is rich and evocative, immersing the reader in a world filled with danger and seduction. The pacing is deliberate, allowing the intricate layers of character relationships to unfold organically. The dual POV structure serves to deepen our understanding of the protagonists’ motivations, yet at times it risks fragmentation, leaving moments of tension feeling a tad drawn out. Notably, the dialogue crackles with energy, reflecting the characters' emotional stakes, though some exchanges occasionally tread too close to melodrama, undermining the otherwise sophisticated narrative voice.

Thematically, **A Strange Hymn** excels in exploring the duality of love as both a source of strength and vulnerability. The dark magic serves as a metaphor for the characters' internal struggles, illustrating how the allure of power can corrupt and complicate genuine connections. However, the ending, while dramatic, feels somewhat rushed, leaving certain character arcs unresolved and potentially polarizing readers on whether the conclusion was earned or a mere setup for further sequels. 

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of love and power within **A Strange Hymn** raises several polarizing questions that can ignite passionate debate:
- How effectively does Thalassa balance the fantasy elements with the emotional depth of her characters?
- Are the darker themes vital to the narrative, or do they overshadow the romantic elements?
- Is the pacing of the character development satisfying, or does it detract from the overall tension?
- How does the dual POV enhance or complicate the reader's connection with the characters?

**Questions for the room:**

- In what ways does this sequel stand apart from the first book in the series?
- How do you interpret the use of dark magic in relation to the protagonists’ development?
