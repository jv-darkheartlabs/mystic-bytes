## Hook & thesis
In *Smile Beach Murder*, Alicia Bessette crafts a cozy mystery steeped in the dark undercurrents of murder and secrets lurking beneath the idyllic facade of the Outer Banks. **Verdict:** This book masquerades as a lighthearted whodunit, yet it grapples with the haunting shadows of grief and betrayal that linger when love is intertwined with loss.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the dark-thriller and cozy mystery genres, Bessette's narrative walks a thin line between charming small-town quirks and the sinister realities of human nature. The author's previous works have often explored themes of loss and love, but here she invites readers who typically shy away from darker themes to dip their toes into the murky waters of suspense. With the bookshop setting serving as both a refuge and a stage for treachery, it appeals to an audience that appreciates the juxtaposition of cozy environments against the brutal backdrop of murder.

## Deep-dive critique
Bessette's pacing is both a strength and a flaw; while the narrative unfolds at a leisurely pace, allowing the reader to savor the atmospheric details of the bookshop and its surroundings, the slow burn may frustrate those seeking immediate thrills. The first-person point of view provides an intimate window into the protagonist's psyche, revealing vulnerabilities and fears that resonate with anyone who has faced the specter of loss. However, this choice also constrains the narrative, limiting the exploration of secondary characters who could have added depth to the protagonist's journey. 

The dialogue is peppered with wit, yet at times, it becomes overly whimsical, detracting from the gravity of the underlying murder plot. This tonal misalignment can create a jarring experience, where the lighthearted banter feels misplaced against the backdrop of grim realities. Bessette's world-building is evocative, painting a vivid picture of the Outer Banks, yet it sometimes glosses over the darker aspects, relying on the reader's imagination to fill in the gaps of despair and isolation that accompany loss. The ending, while satisfying in wrapping up the mystery, feels somewhat rushed and lacks the emotional resonance that the build-up promises.

## Discussion launchpad
There are polarities lurking within *Smile Beach Murder* that could ignite passionate debate among readers. Is it a vibrant exploration of love and loss, or does its failure to fully embrace its darker side render it less impactful? 

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the cozy mystery genre adequately handle themes of grief, or does it dilute the seriousness of such themes?  
- How effective is Bessette's use of humor in the face of dark subject matter — does it enhance or detract from the narrative?  
- In what ways could the secondary characters have been developed to enrich the protagonist’s story?  
- Does the pacing of the book serve to build tension, or does it undermine the suspense?
