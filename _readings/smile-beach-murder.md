## Hook & thesis
In *Smile Beach Murder*, Alicia Bessette crafts a darkly whimsical mystery where the sunlit façade of beach life masks deeper truths of human frailty and deceit. **Verdict:** This novel cleverly juxtaposes the charm of a coastal bookshop with sinister undercurrents, revealing human connections that are as fragile as sandcastles.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the cozy mystery genre, Bessette’s work embraces familiar tropes while threading a darker, more psychological exploration of relationships and trust. As part of the Outer Banks Bookshop Mystery series, it harks back to the likes of Tana French and Louise Penny, yet with a distinctly lighter, yet unnerving tone. The invisible audience here spans both cozy mystery enthusiasts and readers drawn to romantic suspense, creating a bridge that invites exploration of deeper themes beneath its cheerful surface.

## Deep-dive critique
Structurally, *Smile Beach Murder* takes an unconventional approach, weaving clues and character arcs with a rhythm that mirrors the ebb and flow of tides. Bessette utilizes a close third-person POV, allowing readers to dwell intimately in the protagonist's psyche while teasing out the broader mystery. This choice propels the narrative, as tension mounts with each revelation, leading to an ending that feels both earned and hauntingly bittersweet.

The prose is light yet evocative, capturing the idyllic setting while imbibing it with an atmosphere thick with suspense. Bessette excels in character development, particularly in her nuanced portrayal of interpersonal dynamics within the seemingly idyllic community. Dialogue flows naturally, revealing layers of tension and desire that pulse beneath the surface. However, the pacing occasionally stumbles, notably in the middle chapters where the focus shifts momentarily from character motivations to the mechanics of mystery, risking reader engagement. 

Despite its cozy trappings, the thematic exploration of trust and betrayal digs into darker territories, questioning the very fabric of human relationships. The resolution complicates the idea of justice, leaving readers with a lingering sense of unease — a hallmark of successful dark romance and thriller narratives.

## Discussion launchpad
*Smile Beach Murder* invites dialogue about the complexities of human relationships against a backdrop of lighthearted mystery. While the book delivers a satisfying conclusion, it also leaves several questions unaddressed regarding morality and the consequences of choices made in the name of love.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the setting of a cozy bookshop enhance or detract from the darker themes explored in the novel?  
- In what ways does the author balance humor and suspense without undermining the emotional stakes?  
- Are the relationships portrayed in the story ultimately redeemable, or do they reflect a more cynical view of human nature?  
- How does the narrative structure influence your perception of the characters’ motivations?  
- What lingering questions do you have about the protagonist's journey or the bookshop's community?
