## Hook & thesis
*Endfall* by David Estes is a gripping exploration of love's dark aftermath, weaving threads of grief and desire into a tapestry that pulses with tension. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully blends fantasy and romance, exposing the raw edges of human connection in a world where the stakes are nothing less than life and death.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled firmly within the fantasy-romance genre, *Endfall* operates in a space reminiscent of works by authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout, where the supernatural intermingles with deeply personal narratives. Estes deftly navigates the classic tropes of dark romance while pushing against the boundaries of conventional storytelling. The invisible audience here appears to be readers seeking not just escape, but an emotional reckoning, drawing in both genre enthusiasts and newcomers alike. The book bridges niche elements—such as morally ambiguous characters and high-stakes romance—to appeal to a broader readership, creating a unique landscape that feels both familiar and fresh.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Endfall* employs a dual narrative, weaving together past and present through alternating perspectives that invite readers to uncover the layers of trauma and longing that bind the characters. This choice of POV enhances the emotional stakes, allowing for a dynamic exploration of how grief can warp love. The pacing is deliberate, almost languorous at times, which serves to heighten the tension and develop the slow burn of attraction amidst chaos. However, it risks alienating readers who prefer more immediate gratification, raising questions about narrative satisfaction.

In terms of craft, Estes excels in world-building, creating a vividly immersive setting that resonates with the characters’ inner turmoil. The prose is rich and evocative, filled with imagery that pulls readers into the visceral nature of the characters' experiences. Dialogues are sharp and layered, revealing motivations and vulnerabilities with each exchange, but occasionally fall into moments of exposition that could benefit from more subtlety. The ending, while cathartic, may leave some feeling unresolved, challenging readers to grapple with the complexities of love and loss instead of providing neat closure.

## Discussion launchpad
*Endfall* invites polarizing discussions around the nature of love in times of despair and the ethical implications of characters' choices. What remains unaddressed in this exploration of dark romance? How do we reconcile the characters' flaws with our desires to empathize with them?

**Questions for the room:**
- How does the dual narrative impact your connection to the characters?
- In what ways does the pacing affect your perception of tension and romance?
- What are your thoughts on the ending? Did it feel earned or abrupt?
- How effectively does Estes balance fantasy elements with real emotional stakes?
- Can you identify moments where exposition undermines character depth?
