## Hook & thesis
In Michael Crichton's **Jurassic Park**, the author artfully explores humanity's hubris against nature, revealing the catastrophic consequences of playing god. **Verdict:** Crichton’s work serves as a dark cautionary tale, where science's triumph over natural order spirals into chaos, echoing the relentless pursuit of control that ultimately leads to destruction.

## The contextual pivot
Situated firmly in the dark-thriller genre, **Jurassic Park** embodies the tension between advancement and ethics—a common motif in Crichton’s oeuvre, where technology often emerges as both savior and harbinger of doom. This novel stands alongside the likes of H.G. Wells’ *The Island of Doctor Moreau* and Mary Shelley’s *Frankenstein*, probing the moral dilemmas intertwined with scientific exploration. The invisible audience here is a blend of science fiction enthusiasts and general readers, as Crichton bridges niche tropes of bioengineering and survival horror with a mainstream appeal that invites all to ponder the thin line between ingenuity and irresponsibility.

## Deep-dive critique
Crichton’s narrative structure—interwoven perspectives and a non-linear timeline—effectively heightens the suspense, echoing the chaotic unraveling of events on Isla Nublar. The pacing is relentless, mirroring the urgency of the characters as they grapple with the consequences of their creations. Prose-wise, Crichton excels in crafting vivid, tense imagery, particularly in scenes of predation that are laden with psychological intensity, reflecting the primal fears that linger beneath the surface of civilization.

Characterization, however, sometimes falters under the weight of exposition; while figures like Dr. Alan Grant and Ian Malcolm are vividly drawn, others lack depth, serving more as vehicles for thematic exploration than as fully realized individuals. The dialogue, often rife with scientific jargon, can alienate readers who are less versed in the language of genetics and chaos theory, creating a barrier to emotional engagement. The ending, though explosive and climactic, feels somewhat unearned, leaving lingering questions about the broader implications of humanity's choices within the narrative’s moral framework. 

## Discussion launchpad
Crichton’s exploration of ethics in science and the consequences of human arrogance presents polarizing questions about our relationship with technology and nature.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of scientific ambition resonate with contemporary issues in genetic engineering?  
- How effectively does the novel balance thrilling action with ethical considerations?  
- In what ways do the character arcs reinforce or detract from the central themes?  
- Can the catastrophic events in *Jurassic Park* be seen as a metaphor for modern technological society?  
- How does Crichton’s treatment of female characters impact the narrative?
