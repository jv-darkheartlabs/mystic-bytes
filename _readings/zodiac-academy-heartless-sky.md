## Hook & thesis
In *Zodiac Academy: Heartless Sky*, Caroline Peckham and Susanne Valenti thrust readers into a world where desire and vengeance intertwine in a dance as dangerous as the magic that fuels it. **Verdict:** The book is an audacious exploration of power dynamics wrapped in a gothic romance that pushes the boundaries of emotional and physical peril.

## The contextual pivot
As the seventh installment in the *Zodiac Academy* series, *Heartless Sky* nestles into the bully-romance genre that thrives on the duality of attraction and antagonism. This series has cultivated a devoted audience with its heady blend of supernatural elements and intensely flawed characters, appealing to readers who relish the thrill of moral ambiguity. In comparison to other authors like C. M. Owens or Anna Zaires, the book successfully straddles niche tropes while remaining accessible to a broader audience, drawing in newcomers eager for a taste of the darker realms of romance.

## Deep-dive critique
Peckham and Valenti’s narrative structure weaves a tapestry of conflict through dual perspectives, enhancing the emotional stakes as the protagonists maneuver through their tumultuous relationships. The pacing is often frenetic, mirroring the characters' emotional instability and the chaotic environment of the academy, which serves both to enthrall and to occasionally overwhelm the reader. While the relentless tension keeps one engaged, there are moments where the rapid shifts in tone can feel disorienting, particularly in scenes that pivot from high-stakes conflict to intimate encounters.

The prose is imbued with visceral imagery that heightens emotional responses, making the underlying themes of grief and longing palpable. Characterization shines, particularly in how the authors navigate the complexities of love born from trauma and betrayal. However, dialogue can occasionally slip into melodrama, diluting the potency of certain exchanges. The world-building is richly detailed, creating a vivid backdrop where the supernatural is not just a flourish but a necessary element of the characters' struggles. The ending, while satisfying in its resolution of certain arcs, leaves much to be desired in terms of character growth, raising questions about the protagonists’ future beyond the confines of their current chaos.

## Discussion launchpad
The intricate dance of desire and danger in *Heartless Sky* ignites polarizing views on the ethics of its romantic entanglements. Does the portrayal of toxic relationships serve to critique or glorify such dynamics? 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do the authors balance the lines between romance and toxicity in character relationships?  
- In what ways does the setting of the academy enhance or detract from the themes of the story?  
- Are the protagonists' actions justifiable within the context of their emotional trauma, or do they perpetuate a harmful narrative?  
- What role does the supernatural play in shaping the characters' moral compasses?  
- How does the resolution impact your perception of the characters, and do you feel it is earned?
