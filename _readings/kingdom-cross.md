## Hook & thesis
In **Kingdom Cross**, Shantel Tessier dives deep into the psyche of obsession, unearthing the tumultuous relationship between desire and destruction. The author’s primary objective is to explore the complexities of love intertwined with violence, showcasing how far one might go when pushed to the brink. **Verdict:** This book is a visceral exploration of the boundaries of love, both beautiful and savage, leaving readers questioning the very nature of possession.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the dark-billionaire subgenre, **Kingdom Cross** intersects with the likes of authors such as Anna Zaires and Pepper Winters, who similarly navigate the treacherous waters of twisted romance and moral ambiguity. Tessier’s work stands out not only for its raw portrayal of desire but also for its inclination towards gothic elements, echoing themes found in classic literature while appealing to a modern audience captivated by taboo narratives. The invisible audience here embraces those drawn to narratives thick with tension and darker human experiences, effectively bridging niche tropes to reach a broader readership that seeks both thrill and introspection.

## Deep-dive critique
Tessier's craft excels remarkably in its exploration of character motivation, particularly through the duality of her protagonists. The pacing is deliberate, mirroring the tension between the characters’ longing and their destructive tendencies. The first-person perspective pulls readers into the chaotic mind of the heroine, allowing an intimate glimpse into her fears and desires, which amplifies the narrative's stakes. This choice of POV heightens the emotional resonance, yet at times, it also risks alienating readers who might find her obsessive tendencies off-putting.

The dialogue crackles with intensity, revealing both vulnerability and a raw edge to the characters' interactions, though occasionally, it veers into melodrama. Tessier skillfully employs vivid imagery, particularly in scenes that evoke the book's darker themes—arson and violence are not just background elements but integral to the characters’ emotional landscapes. However, the ending, while impactful, raises questions about whether the emotional arcs feel fully resolved, leaving a lingering sense of disquiet that may resonate differently with readers. This ambiguity serves the thematic exploration of obsession but risks frustrating those seeking closure.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay between love and violence in **Kingdom Cross** poses provocative questions about the nature of romance and the boundaries of acceptable behavior in relationships. The book's portrayal of possessive love may spark varied interpretations, eliciting both admiration and discomfort.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do we reconcile the romanticized portrayal of obsession with its darker implications?  
- Do we find the characters relatable or merely cautionary tales?  
- What is the impact of the first-person narrative on our empathy for the characters?  
- How do the themes of violence and desire reflect contemporary societal issues?  
- Is the ending successful in conveying the intended themes, or does it fall short?
