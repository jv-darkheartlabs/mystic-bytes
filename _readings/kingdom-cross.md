## Hook & thesis
Shantel Tessier’s *Kingdom Cross* subverts the dark billionaire trope with a visceral exploration of obsession and sacrifice wrapped in a seductive veneer. **Verdict:** This novel sears through the lush trappings of wealth to reveal the fragile threads binding love and destruction.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned squarely within the dark romance genre, *Kingdom Cross* fuses the billionaire archetype with gothic elements, echoing the likes of Anna Zaires and Skye Warren, while threading a unique narrative that amplifies the stakes of desire. Tessier deftly navigates the delicate balance between niche tropes and broader themes of power dynamics and emotional turmoil, appealing to both seasoned dark romance aficionados and curious general readers alike. The palpable intensity of the protagonists' entanglement invites deeper reflections on the nature of love intertwined with danger, making it a potential cornerstone in Tessier’s oeuvre.

## Deep-dive critique
Tessier employs a dual POV structure, allowing readers to delve into the minds of both the brooding alpha and the resilient heroine. This choice heightens the tension, particularly as the dual perspectives contrast their motivations and vulnerabilities. The pacing, however, occasionally falters; while the buildup to climactic moments generates anticipation, some transitions feel rushed, potentially leaving readers yearning for deeper exploration of character backstories and motivations. 

The prose is rich and evocative, with a gothic undercurrent that complements the opulence of the billionaire's world, yet it leans into melodrama at times, which may alienate those preferring subtlety. Dialogue crackles with tension, effectively capturing the charged atmosphere between characters—each word calculated, each silence pregnant with meaning.

The thematic success lies in Tessier's ability to illustrate the cost of obsession, encapsulating the struggle between desire and self-destruction. However, the resolution leaves lingering questions about the characters' growth; the ending, while climactic, teeters on the edge of predictability, begging for a more radical transformation for its protagonists.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of wealth, power, and emotional vulnerability creates a fertile ground for debate. How effectively does Tessier navigate the fine line between romantic desire and toxic obsession? The novel's portrayal of dominance and submission may polarize readers, raising questions about consent and power dynamics in relationships.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the dual POV enhance or detract from the overall narrative?  
- In what ways does the novel challenge or reinforce traditional dark romance tropes?  
- Do you find the characters’ journeys believable, and how does the ending impact your perception of their evolution?  
- Is the portrayal of wealth and power a meaningful commentary or a mere backdrop for romance?  
- How do the elements of gothic romance play a role in shaping the characters’ desires and conflicts?
