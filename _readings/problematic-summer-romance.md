## Hook & thesis
In *Problematic Summer Romance*, Ali Hazelwood crafts an electrifying exploration of rivalry transformed into passion, revealing the complexities of trust and vulnerability within a fiercely competitive landscape. **Verdict:** This novel navigates the treacherous waters of desire and intellect with an incisive wit that leaves readers both breathless and contemplative.

## The contextual pivot
Hazelwood's work fits squarely within the enemies-to-lovers subgenre, a beloved trope that thrives on tension and the clash of opposing forces. Her insertion of STEM elements adds a refreshing layer, distinguishing her from traditional romantic authors and situating her among contemporaries like Talia Hibbert and Christina Lauren, who similarly blend humor with deeper emotional currents. This book targets readers who revel in smart romance, appealing to both niche audiences and general readers who appreciate the thrill of intellectual sparring intertwined with romance.

## Deep-dive critique
Crafted with an incisive narrative style, *Problematic Summer Romance* excels in its character development and dialogue, using sharp repartee to signal the underlying attraction between the two leads. The alternating perspectives keep the reader engaged, allowing for a deeper understanding of both characters' motivations, which serves to heighten the stakes of their romance. However, the pacing occasionally falters, particularly in the mid-section, where the tension dips as external conflicts momentarily overshadow the intimate allure of their rivalry.

The thematic exploration of trust is particularly resonant; as the protagonists grapple with their competitive instincts, the novel poses poignant questions about the authenticity of their feelings. The world-building within a STEM-focused environment adds depth, yet at times risks alienating readers unfamiliar with the jargon. Still, the ending is satisfyingly earned, culminating in a moment that feels both inevitable and hard-won, reinforcing the idea that true connection often arises from the ashes of conflict.

## Discussion launchpad
Hazelwood’s nuanced handling of rivalry and romance introduces polarizing elements regarding the portrayal of ambition, gender dynamics, and how they intersect within romantic narratives. While some readers may revel in the complexity and humor, others might find the depiction of competitive relationships problematic or even unromantic.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do you feel about the portrayal of ambition in romantic relationships? Does it enhance or detract from the emotional connection?  
- In what ways does the STEM setting serve the narrative, and does it risk exclusion for some readers?  
- Can humor coexist with deeper themes of trust and vulnerability, or does it undermine the seriousness of the characters’ journey?  
- What are your thoughts on the pacing — did it contribute to or hinder your connection with the characters?  
- How does the ending shape your perception of the protagonists’ relationship moving forward?
