## Hook & thesis
In *Godkiller*, Hannah Kaner crafts a dark tapestry of desire and defiance, exploring the emotional and physical toll of a world steeped in violence and divine betrayal. **Verdict:** This novel is a visceral exploration of love's capacity for destruction, balancing the fantastical with raw human emotion in a way that is both intoxicating and unsettling.

## The contextual pivot
As a work of fantasy-romance, *Godkiller* occupies a unique space within the genre, merging the visceral stakes of dark fantasy with the intimate dynamics of romantic entanglements. Kaner deftly navigates the tropes of god-killers and their complex relationships with the divine, echoing themes found in the works of authors like Sarah J. Maas and Leigh Bardugo. However, unlike the often melodramatic tendencies of her peers, Kaner layers her narrative with a gritty realism that makes the emotional stakes feel immediate and urgent. The book seems poised to appeal not only to niche fantasy enthusiasts but also to general readers who crave depth in their romantic encounters.

## Deep-dive critique
Kaner’s prose is sharp and evocative, with a pacing that mirrors the tension of her characters' struggles. The narrative unfolds through a dual perspective, allowing readers to intimately engage with both protagonists as they navigate their tumultuous relationship against a backdrop of societal upheaval. This structure enhances the thematic exploration of loyalty and betrayal, as the characters grapple with their feelings in a world where gods can be both protectors and destroyers.

The world-building stands out for its richness; Kaner vividly depicts a landscape scarred by divine conflict, imbuing it with history and lore that feel lived-in rather than mere decoration. Yet, the emotional intensity occasionally overshadows the plot, leading to moments where the pacing falters, particularly in the midsection. Some scenes, while beautifully written, linger too long on introspection, risking the readers' engagement at critical turning points. 

The dialogue is another strong suit, crackling with tension and imbued with an underlying sense of longing that propels the narrative forward. Kaner’s characters are flawed and complex, navigating their desires and resentments with a raw honesty that resonates. Yet, the ending, while climactic, raises questions about its earnestly resolved arcs. It feels as though the journey toward resolution, fraught with so much emotional investment, may have left certain threads tantalizingly unresolved.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay between love and violence is at the heart of *Godkiller*, provoking thought about the nature of desire and sacrifice in the context of a brutal world. This duality may spark divergent opinions among readers. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Kaner balance the romantic elements with the darker themes of violence and betrayal?  
- In what ways do the dual perspectives enhance or detract from the story’s emotional impact?  
- Do you feel the ending was satisfactory, or did it leave unresolved tensions that undermine the character arcs?  
- How can we reconcile the appeal of flawed characters who engage in morally ambiguous actions?  
- What does the portrayal of divine beings in this narrative say about human nature and relationships?
