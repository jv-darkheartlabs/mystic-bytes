## Hook & thesis
In *Godkiller*, Hannah Kaner carves a darkly enchanting narrative where desire and morality intertwine in a treacherous dance of power. **Verdict:** This novel wields its gothic romance with precision, inviting readers to explore the seductive allure of the forbidden and the consequences of divine ambition.

## The contextual pivot
Situated firmly within the realm of fantasy romance, *Godkiller* weaves together themes of divine conflict and human frailty, echoing the works of authors like Sarah J. Maas and V.E. Schwab while carving its own niche. Kaner’s exploration of gods and the mortals who challenge them resonates with a hidden audience that craves the delicious tension of love entwined with danger. By bridging dark romance with fantastical elements, the book straddles the line between niche appeal and broader accessibility, inviting readers from both realms to linger in its shadowy embrace.

## Deep-dive critique
Kaner’s prose is lush, a tapestry of vivid imagery and intricate world-building that envelops the reader in its dark embrace. The pacing propels us through a series of moral dilemmas and passionate encounters, yet it occasionally falters under the weight of exposition that risks bogging down the narrative’s flow. The dual perspectives employed offer an intimate glimpse into the characters’ psyches, yet at times, they create a dissonance that can detract from the urgency of their intertwined fates. 

The thematic exploration of desire as both a weapon and a weakness is masterfully executed — characters grapple not only with their feelings but also with the implications of their choices in a world where gods are not merely distant entities but a visceral presence. The dialogue sparkles with tension, encapsulating the characters' conflicting desires while also grounding their motivations in a relatable human experience. However, the ending, while dramatic, risks leaving some emotional arcs unfulfilled, challenging the reader to ponder the true cost of their desires. 

## Discussion launchpad
*Godkiller* is a rich tapestry that invites debate around the ethics of desire, the nature of power, and the allure of the forbidden. It raises questions about the motivations of its morally ambiguous characters and the implications of their actions in a world governed by divine beings. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Kaner’s portrayal of gods as fallible influence your perception of the characters?  
- In what ways does the tension between duty and desire reflect our own societal norms?  
- Can you reconcile the characters' morally gray choices with the concept of true love?  
- Does the narrative ultimately favor redemption or retribution?  
- How does the structure of dual perspectives enhance or detract from the emotional stakes of the story?
