## Hook & thesis
*The Light We Lost* by Jill Santopolo intricately unravels the complexities of love and choice, probing the emotional labyrinth between desire and regret. **Verdict:** A haunting exploration of forbidden love that masterfully captures the bittersweet essence of choices that define our lives.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled firmly within the realm of forbidden love, Santopolo's work stands out as a poignant examination of desire against the backdrop of personal sacrifice. This novel speaks to readers who relish emotional depth and moral ambiguity, akin to the works of authors like Colleen Hoover and Taylor Jenkins Reid, but with a sharper, darker edge. Santopolo's ability to weave intricate emotional conflicts aims not just at genre enthusiasts but also beckons a broader audience who might be seeking an emotional experience layered with the complexities of modern relationships.

## Deep-dive critique
The narrative structure of *The Light We Lost* employs a dual timeline, allowing readers to oscillate between pivotal moments in the protagonists' lives. This choice effectively enhances the tension as we witness the evolution of their forbidden love. The first-person point of view immerses us deeply into the protagonist’s psyche, crafting an intimate connection that underscores the novel's core theme: the haunting nature of choices. Santopolo's prose is both lyrical and vivid, painting a rich tapestry of New York City that feels alive, echoing the emotional stakes at play.

However, the pacing occasionally falters, particularly in the middle sections, where the emotional intensity threatens to lose momentum, briefly diluting the urgency of the narrative. Dialogue shines in moments of revelation, though some exchanges feel contrived, distancing the reader from the raw emotions that the plot seeks to evoke. The ending, while powerful, leaves an ambivalent taste — successfully echoing the novel's themes but also raising questions about the resolution and the characters’ future paths.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of love and choice creates a fertile ground for discussion, as the novel's exploration of what it means to choose one love over another can be polarizing. What does it say about our own desires when faced with similar choices? The moral ambiguity challenges readers to reflect on their beliefs surrounding fidelity and sacrifice.

**Questions for the room:**
- How do you perceive the moral implications of the protagonist's choices? 
- What does the dual timeline structure add to your understanding of love and loss? 
- In what ways does New York City function as a character in the novel?
- How do moments of dialogue either build or break the emotional connection with the characters?
- Is the ending satisfying, or does it leave too much unresolved?
