## Hook & thesis
In S. Massery's **Brutal Obsession**, the narrative navigates the treacherous waters of desire and the consuming nature of obsession, ultimately revealing the dark underbelly of love that transcends the boundaries of passion and possession. **Verdict:** This book is a provocative exploration of the duality of love and control, leaving readers breathless and slightly unsettled.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned firmly within the stalker-obsession subgenre, **Brutal Obsession** echoes themes found in the works of authors like Tarryn Fisher and Anna Zaires, who similarly delve into the complexities of dark romance. Massery’s narrative bridges niche tropes—such as the power imbalance and the seductive allure of danger—with elements that appeal to a broader audience, inviting readers who may be less familiar with the genre to ponder the fine line between love and obsession. This novel is not merely a tale of hockey prowess and ballet elegance; it is an intricate exploration of how desire can warp into something dangerously addictive.

## Deep-dive critique
Structurally, Massery employs a dual POV that enriches the reader's understanding of both main characters’ internal struggles, allowing for a nuanced portrayal of their obsession. The pacing is deliberate, drawing the reader into the spiraling intensity of their connection while balancing the moments of tension with quieter reflections that reveal character vulnerabilities. However, at times, the shifts in timeline can feel abrupt, potentially jarring the reader from the immersive experience the narrative strives to create. 

Massery's prose fluctuates between lyrical and stark, effectively mirroring the tumultuous emotional landscape of the characters. Her dialogue crackles with an authenticity that captures the tension and chemistry between the protagonists, though certain exchanges can veer towards melodrama, slightly undermining the otherwise compelling character dynamics. The world-building, while visceral, could benefit from deeper exploration of the characters' environments, particularly the juxtaposition of the competitive sports world against the delicate artistry of ballet, which could have served as a richer metaphor for their conflicting desires.

The culmination of the narrative leads to an ending that, while shocking, feels both earned and unsettling. Massery does not shy away from confronting the darker ramifications of obsession, forcing readers to grapple with their own feelings of empathy and repulsion towards the characters’ choices.

## Discussion launchpad
**Brutal Obsession** is rife with polarities that could ignite fervent discussions around the nature of desire and the ethical lines of romantic relationships. How do we navigate the distinction between intense passion and harmful obsession? 

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of obsession in the novel elicit sympathy, or does it simply reinforce harmful stereotypes?  
- How does Massery balance the thrill of danger with the ethical implications of obsessive love?  
- In what ways do the worlds of hockey and ballet enhance or detract from the central themes of the novel?  
- Can the characters’ actions ever be justified, and what does that say about our own responses to obsession in literature?  
- How does the dual POV shape our understanding of love and control?
