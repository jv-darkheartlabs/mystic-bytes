## Hook & thesis
In *A City of Emeralds and Envy*, Rebecca F. Kenney artfully intertwines the seductive allure of dark fantasy with the piercing sting of unrequited desire. **Verdict:** This volume transcends its retelling roots, emerging as a poignant exploration of envy that ensnares both heart and mind.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled within the burgeoning subgenre of fantasy romance, Kenney’s work stands as a testament to the complex narratives that can arise from the retelling of classic tales. This book, the third installment in the *Wicked Darlings* series, draws on the rich tapestry of *The Wizard of Oz*, but notably diverges from its whimsical origins, opting instead for a more gothic ambiance colored by themes of longing and vengeance. Kenney’s established audience, familiar with her penchant for intertwining love with darker elements, will find solace in her intricate world-building and character arcs. Yet, this installment possesses the potential to bridge niche tropes to general readers who crave depth behind the surface allure of fantasy romance.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *A City of Emeralds and Envy* is both fluid and calculated, utilizing a dual POV that allows readers to traverse the contrasting landscapes of desire and betrayal. The pacing is deliberate, yet it deftly escalates tension, particularly in scenes suffused with palpable longing and jealousy. Kenney’s prose sings with a lush, evocative quality, crafting a world where emerald foliage and blood-red poppies become metaphors for the story's darker themes. Character development is particularly strong; the protagonists are imbued with complexity that invites sympathy even in their most misguided actions. Dialogue sparkles with wit and tension, propelling the plot while illuminating the intricacies of each character's motivations. However, the narrative falters at times when the weight of environmental description overshadows character progression, momentarily stifling the momentum. The ending, while satisfying, leaves a lingering sense of an unresolved tension, mirroring the central conflict of envy that permeates the narrative.

## Discussion launchpad
While Kenney's lush prose and intricate character dynamics provoke admiration, there are polarities that may ignite debate among readers. The balance between fantasy elements and emotional depth could be considered one of the book's strengths or a potential detriment, depending on personal taste. Furthermore, the exploration of envy as a driving force raises questions about morality and desire. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do you perceive the balance of fantasy versus emotional realism in this narrative?  
- Do you believe the portrayal of envy was effective in driving the characters' arcs, or did it undermine their growth?  
- In what ways does this retelling challenge or reinforce traditional tropes within the fantasy romance genre?  
- How do you interpret the unresolved tensions in the ending — is it a narrative strength or a flaw?  
- What role does the lush description play in your connection to the characters and their journeys?
