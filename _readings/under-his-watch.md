## Hook & thesis
*Under His Watch* by Leah D. Cummings expertly navigates the treacherous waters of obsession and surveillance, unveiling the chilling dynamics of power in intimacy. **Verdict:** This book is a dark exploration of desire and control, masterfully entangling readers in its web of psychological tension.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled firmly in the stalker-obsession subgenre of dark romance, Cummings’ work draws on the gothic undercurrents of surveillance and predation. Comparable to the unsettling narratives of authors like Tarryn Fisher and the psychological grip of *You* by Caroline Kepnes, this title speaks to a niche audience hungry for stories that blur the lines between love and violation. The invisible readership—those who revel in the darker facets of romance—will find this novel both provocative and unsettling, while it also has the potential to resonate beyond its niche by appealing to readers who appreciate psychological depth and character complexity.

## Deep-dive critique
Cummings employs a third-person limited omniscient point of view that draws readers into the protagonist’s psyche while maintaining a necessary distance from the object of obsession. This choice heightens the tension, as the audience becomes acutely aware of the protagonist's internal conflicts. The pacing, marked by a deliberate build-up, mirrors the protagonist’s descent into obsession, allowing for moments of breathless anticipation that accentuate the darker themes. The prose is rich with metaphor and imagery, particularly the recurring motif of the spider and its web, which serves not only as a symbol of entrapment but also hints at the delicate balance between attraction and danger. 

However, the structure falters slightly in its climax, which, while intense, feels somewhat rushed compared to the meticulous buildup. The dialogue, at times sharp and revealing, could benefit from further nuance, as certain exchanges risk becoming predictable. Yet, the thematic exploration of surveillance as an act of both longing and control remains hauntingly effective. The ending, while cathartic, leaves lingering questions about accountability and the nature of love, inviting readers to ponder the implications of such obsessions.

## Discussion launchpad
Cummings’ narrative pushes boundaries, eliciting a spectrum of responses regarding morality and love. The interplay of desire and control, as well as the normalization of surveillance in modern relationships, can spark heated debates. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the portrayal of obsession challenge or reinforce societal views on love?  
- In what ways do you think the narrative reflects contemporary issues regarding privacy and technology?  
- Does the protagonist's journey evoke empathy or condemnation, and why?  
- Can true love exist within the confines of obsession, or is it inherently tainted?  
- What are the implications of the book's ending on our understanding of the characters' morality?
