## Hook & thesis
In Leah D. Cummings' *Under His Watch*, the chilling interplay of obsession and surveillance unveils the dark recesses of desire, challenging the reader to navigate the thin line between love and possession. **Verdict:** This novel is a disturbing meditation on control masquerading as intimacy, and it succeeds in holding a mirror to our own vulnerabilities.

## The contextual pivot
*Under His Watch* finds its place within the stalker-obsession subgenre, a niche that thrives on the tension between predator and prey. Cummings deftly combines elements of gothic romance with contemporary anxieties surrounding technology and privacy, echoing works by authors like C.D. Reiss and Talia Hibbert, yet infusing her narrative with a uniquely unsettling energy. The invisible audience here is likely those who revel in dark romance's psychological intensity, and Cummings bridges the gap by employing familiar tropes while introducing an unsettling realism that can resonate with general readers who grapple with themes of autonomy and desire.

## Deep-dive critique
Cummings' narrative structure employs a dual point of view, alternating between the perspectives of the stalker and the stalked, which effectively serves the thesis by illuminating the distorted lenses through which love and obsession are perceived. This choice creates a tension that escalates throughout the story, capturing the reader's attention while simultaneously invoking discomfort. The pacing is deliberate; it mirrors the slow, suffocating descent into obsession, revealing character motivations organically rather than forcing plot progression. 

The prose is sharp and evocative, often juxtaposing lyrical descriptions of the protagonist's inner turmoil with stark, unsettling imagery of surveillance. Cummings excels in world-building, crafting a contemporary landscape fraught with technological intrusion that enhances the gothic undertones of the narrative. However, there are moments where dialogue feels contrived, as if to hastily advance the plot rather than serve character development, which somewhat undercuts the overall tension. 

The ending, while inevitable, is both earned and jarring, forcing the reader to confront the implications of unchecked obsession. It lingers, leaving behind an unsettling residue that compels further reflection on the nature of desire and agency.

## Discussion launchpad
The polarizing elements of this novel lie within its ethical considerations and the portrayal of obsession. Cummings invites readers to grapple with uncomfortable truths about desire, but the question remains: where does admiration cross into dangerous territory?
- **Questions for the room:**  
  - Does the dual perspective enhance or detract from the narrative's psychological depth?  
  - In what ways does Cummings' portrayal of technology as a tool for obsession resonate with contemporary societal fears?  
  - How do we reconcile the romanticized depiction of obsession with the reality of stalking?  
  - What role does the setting play in amplifying the themes of control and captivity?  
  - Can a story that delves into such dark themes still provide a fulfilling romantic arc, or does it inherently undermine the notion of healthy relationships?
