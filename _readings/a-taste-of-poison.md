## Hook & thesis
*A Taste of Poison* by Tessonja Odette crafts a lush, shadowy retelling of Snow White that dives deep into the heart of desire and betrayal. **Verdict:** This tale tantalizes with its exploration of the intoxicating allure of the forbidden, revealing that true danger often resides in the sweetest temptations.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled within the fantasy-romance genre, *A Taste of Poison* harmonizes elements of dark romance with fairy tale nostalgia. Odette's work stands as a vibrant addition to the landscape of reimagined classics, drawing comparisons to the intricate world-building of Sarah J. Maas and the rich, atmospheric prose of Holly Black. The book serves an audience that craves both the thrill of fae enchantment and the darker undertones of desire, merging niche tropes with broader romantic themes to create an enticing read for both genre aficionados and newcomers alike.

## Deep-dive critique
Odette's structural choices serve to heighten the tension throughout the narrative. The dual perspectives offer a nuanced glimpse into the motivations of both the protagonist and her fae counterpart, allowing readers to oscillate between empathy and suspicion. This shifting point of view is not merely a stylistic choice but a necessary device to illuminate the complexities of trust and desire amid betrayal. 

The pacing, particularly in the first half, oscillates gracefully between moments of lush description and sharp tension, embodying the themes of sweetness and poison. The dialogue carries an electric charge, often laden with subtext, which enhances the stakes at play. Odette’s prose evokes a haunting beauty, where even the most seemingly innocuous moments pulse with an undercurrent of danger. Yet, at times, the narrative can feel over-embellished, risking clarity for lyrical flourish, which may alienate readers seeking a more straightforward tale.

As the climax unfolds, the ending attempts to tie together the themes of sacrifice and desire, yet it straddles the line between satisfying and abrupt. While it offers resolution, some readers may find themselves yearning for a deeper exploration of the repercussions of the characters' decisions. The thematic success lies in its representation of the alluring yet perilous dance of love and betrayal, but the execution falters slightly in delivering a truly earned conclusion.

## Discussion launchpad
This retelling leaves room for spirited debate, particularly in how it navigates the boundaries between love and manipulation. The themes of toxic love versus genuine connection are layered, provoking contemplation on the nature of desire itself.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of the fae challenge or reinforce traditional tropes of power dynamics in romance?  
- How effectively does the dual perspective enhance or detract from the central conflict?  
- Can we separate the allure of the sweet from the underlying poison it carries?  
- What does this retelling reveal about the nature of fairy tales in modern storytelling?  
- In what ways does the ending resonate (or not) with the themes established throughout the novel?
