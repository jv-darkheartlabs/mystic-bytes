## Hook & thesis
*The Rivaled Crown* by Holly Renee immerses readers in a world where desire and ambition clash, ultimately revealing the human heart's insatiable hunger for power and connection. **Verdict:** This book deftly explores the murky waters of love and rivalry, leaving a lingering sense of unease that keeps one questioning the cost of desire.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled in the realm of fantasy-romance, *The Rivaled Crown* straddles the line between escapism and the darker intricacies of human relationships. Renee's work resonates with fans of authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout, yet it possesses a unique edge that delves deeper into the moral ambiguities of its characters. The book appeals not only to avid romantics but also to those who appreciate a narrative rich with tension and shadows, bridging niche tropes with broader themes of power and betrayal.

## Deep-dive critique
Renee employs a dual POV structure that grants readers insight into the minds of both protagonists, allowing for a nuanced exploration of their motivations and desires. This approach, however, comes with its pitfalls; at times, the shifting perspectives disrupt the pacing, leading to moments where the tension dissipates instead of building. The prose is lush and evocative, painting vivid imagery that complements the emotional landscape, though it occasionally veers into melodrama, which may alienate some readers seeking a more restrained narrative.

Character development is one of the book's strongest elements, as the protagonists grapple with their ambitions while confronting their vulnerabilities. Dialogues crackle with intensity, showcasing both the chemistry and animosity that characterize their relationship. Yet, certain supporting characters lack depth, serving more as plot devices than fully realized individuals — a missed opportunity that could have enriched the world-building and added layers to the narrative.

The ending, while satisfying, leaves some threads tantalizingly unresolved, inviting speculation regarding the consequences of the protagonists' choices. This ambiguity serves the book well, reflecting the theme of ambition's price, though it may frustrate readers seeking closure.

## Discussion launchpad
Renee's *The Rivaled Crown* invites a multitude of interpretations, particularly regarding the ethics of desire and the lengths one will go to for power. The tensions between love and rivalry raise questions that linger long after the final page.

**Questions for the room:**
- How do the dual POVs enhance or detract from the narrative's tension?
- What role does the concept of ambition play in shaping the characters' decisions and relationships?
- Are the supporting characters adequately fleshed out to serve their purpose, or do they detract from the main story?
- How effectively does the ending encapsulate the book's themes, and does it leave the reader with a sense of fulfillment or frustration?
- Where does *The Rivaled Crown* fit within the broader context of dark romance in fantasy literature?
