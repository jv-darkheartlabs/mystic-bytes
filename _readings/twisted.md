## Hook & thesis
In *Twisted*, Emily McIntire plunges readers into a world where desire weaves intricately with danger, revealing the seductive allure of forbidden love amid the dark shadows of a villain’s heart. **Verdict:** A masterful exploration of love's capacity for both salvation and destruction, this novel grips the reader with its lush prose and complex characters.

## The contextual pivot
As a part of the *Never After* series, *Twisted* sits comfortably within the realm of dark romance, a genre known for its exploration of taboo relationships and morally ambiguous characters. McIntire, known for her penchant for reimagining classic tales through a modern lens, delivers a provocative narrative that positions itself among the likes of Sarah J. Maas and Cole McCade, drawing on familiar archetypes while subverting expectations. The book's invisible audience comprises readers who are not only fans of forbidden love but also those who appreciate the nuances of villainy and redemption, a daring bridge that invites both niche aficionados and general readers into its depths.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Twisted* is both entrancing and disorienting, employing a dual POV that heightens the sense of intimacy between the protagonists while simultaneously allowing for a rich exploration of their inner turmoils. McIntire's pacing is deliberate, building tension through a slow burn that entwines the characters’ desires and fears, culminating in moments that feel both inevitable and shocking. The dialogue crackles with intensity, each exchange a tantalizing dance of power dynamics that keeps the reader on edge.

However, while the world-building is lush and vivid, there are moments where the exposition feels heavy-handed, momentarily pulling the reader from the emotional gravity of the narrative. Yet, the thematic exploration of love as a means of both corruption and liberation is executed with remarkable finesse, ultimately leading to an ending that, while not conventionally ‘happy,’ resonates deeply with the story’s overarching theme of redemption through love. The conclusion is earned, leaving the reader to ponder the fine line between passion and peril.

## Discussion launchpad
*Twisted* is rife with polarities that could ignite heated debates among readers: the allure of the villain versus the moral implications of loving one, the nature of redemption, and the boundaries of desire. The narrative invites reflection on what constitutes true love when entwined with darkness, leaving several questions unaddressed.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of the villain romanticize toxic traits, or does it offer a nuanced view of redemption?  
- How does McIntire's portrayal of desire challenge or conform to genre conventions?  
- What role does power play in the relationships depicted in the story?  
- Can love truly redeem a villain, or is it merely a façade for their darker inclinations?  
- How do you feel about the ending—was it satisfying or provocative in its ambiguity?
