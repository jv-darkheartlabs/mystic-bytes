## Hook & thesis
*The Goal* by Elle Kennedy serves as both a culmination of the Off-Campus series and a standalone exploration of the tensions inherent in an enemies-to-lovers narrative. **Verdict:** Kennedy expertly navigates the complexities of desire and rivalry, crafting a tale that is as exhilarating as it is tumultuous.

## The contextual pivot
This book situates itself firmly within the realm of contemporary romance, particularly the college and sports subgenres. Kennedy, recognized for her sharp wit and compelling character dynamics, transcends the typical tropes associated with enemies-to-lovers stories. Instead of merely relying on romantic conflict, she delves into the deeper struggles of identity and belonging, appealing to both niche audiences and broader readers alike. The invisible audience here encompasses those who relish both the adrenaline of sports and the richness of character-driven narratives, making it a bridge between dedicated fans of dark romance and those seeking a lighter touch.

## Deep-dive critique
Kennedy's narrative structure deftly intertwines dual perspectives, allowing readers to experience the internal conflicts of both protagonists, Tucker and Sabrina. This choice enhances the emotional stakes, as the alternating POVs provide a nuanced view of their evolving relationship. Pacing is meticulously controlled; moments of tension are interspersed with reflective interludes that deepen character development without losing momentum. The dialogue crackles with a mix of humor and tension, showcasing Kennedy's mastery at crafting witty banter that simultaneously reveals vulnerabilities and desires.

However, the thematic exploration of consequences—particularly surrounding unexpected pregnancy—adds a layer of complexity that elevates the narrative. While the resolution may feel somewhat conventional, the journey to that endpoint is anything but predictable, filled with moments that challenge both characters’ perceptions of love and responsibility. The ending is a bittersweet culmination that feels earned, even as it leaves some threads tantalizingly unresolved, inviting reflection on the nature of relationships forged in adversity.

## Discussion launchpad
Kennedy's work raises pertinent questions about the balance between romantic idealism and the harsh realities of life. The tension between emotional fulfillment and personal growth creates a rich landscape for discussion. What remains unaddressed is the extent to which these characters’ motivations are shaped by societal expectations versus personal desires. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Kennedy’s portrayal of rivalry shape the romantic tension?  
- In what ways does the theme of unexpected consequences enhance or detract from the romance?  
- Do you find the resolution satisfying, or does it feel too neatly tied?  
- How does the dual POV impact your connection to each character?  
- What role does the hockey backdrop play in the emotional stakes of the story?
