## Hook & thesis
*Still Beating* by Jennifer Hartmann plunges readers into the depths of captivity, where survival and desire intertwine in a harrowing dance. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully explores the resilience of the human spirit through the lens of trauma, infusing the captor-captive trope with a chilling authenticity that lingers long after the last page.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled firmly within the dark romance genre, *Still Beating* transcends typical narrative confines, positioning itself alongside works by authors such as Pepper Winters and Keri Lake, who similarly probe the darker recesses of love and survival. Hartmann's narrative grapples with emotional and psychological trauma, making it a potent read for those seeking depth alongside their darker fantasies. The invisible audience likely comprises readers familiar with the nuances of captivity dynamics, yet Hartmann invites a broader spectrum by imbuing her characters with relatable vulnerabilities that resonate beyond niche boundaries.

## Deep-dive critique
Hartmann's craft shines in her deft handling of structure. The nonlinear timeline effectively mirrors the psychological disarray of the characters, creating a palpable tension that underscores the urgency of their plight. The dual POV offers intimacy; readers are thrust into the minds of both captor and captive, revealing layers of conflict and desire that might otherwise remain hidden. This narrative choice not only enriches character development but also challenges readers to grapple with their own moral ambiguities toward love and dominance. 

Dialogue is another stronghold of Hartmann's prose, infused with sharp realism that echoes the characters' emotional states. Exchanges are laced with the kind of raw honesty that escalates tension while fostering an uneasy connection between the leads. However, the pacing occasionally stumbles, particularly in the middle sections where the weight of trauma threatens to overshadow character agency. Despite this, the ending emerges as a triumph, a cathartic release that feels both earned and haunting, leaving an indelible mark of ambiguity about healing and redemption.

## Discussion launchpad
Hartmann’s exploration of trauma as a backdrop for intimacy raises complex questions about consent and survival, polarizing readers between those who find beauty in darkness and those who recoil at the implications of love born from captivity. The unsaid elements of forgiveness, morality, and the nature of desire in trauma-laden relationships beckon deeper scrutiny.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the dual POV enrich or complicate the reader's perspective on the captor-captive dynamic?  
- In what ways does Hartmann challenge the traditional tropes of dark romance?  
- Can love truly emerge from such darkness, or does it perpetuate a cycle of trauma?  
- What role does forgiveness play in the characters' journeys toward healing?  
- How effectively does the setting amplify the psychological stakes of the narrative?
