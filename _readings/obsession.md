# Obsession by Shantel Tessier

## Hook & thesis
In *Obsession*, Shantel Tessier crafts a harrowing tale of desire that spirals into the abyss of possession, challenging the very nature of love and obsession. **Verdict:** This is a visceral exploration of the human soul’s dark corners, where yearning becomes a weapon in a twisted game of dominance and submission.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the stalker-obsession subgenre, *Obsession* navigates a treacherous path through the murky waters of passion and madness. Tessier's narrative echoes the psychological intensity found in works by authors like Tarryn Fisher and Anna Zaires, yet it carves out its own unsettling niche by intertwining elements of the Seven Deadly Sins. Here, the invisible audience comprises readers who revel in the precarious balance of morality and desire, bridging niche tropes to attract those with a taste for darker themes and complex emotional landscapes. Tessier’s work, though a sequel, stands alone in its exploration of obsession's consuming nature, drawing in both genre aficionados and newcomers alike.

## Deep-dive critique
Tessier employs a first-person POV that plunges readers into the psyche of an unreliable narrator, effectively amplifying the tension and uncertainty surrounding the central conflict. The fragmented timeline serves to mirror the protagonist’s spiraling mind, creating a disorienting yet compelling experience that keeps readers on edge. The pacing is meticulously crafted, oscillating between breathless moments of passion and chilling pauses that echo the protagonist’s internal turmoil. 

Dialogue crackles with intensity, revealing the characters’ desires and fears while subtly exposing their vulnerabilities. Tessier’s prose is both raw and poetic, often juxtaposing beauty and horror to accentuate the duality of love and obsession. However, the ending, while dramatic, could be seen as divisive; it leaves the reader grappling with the implications of the characters’ choices, blurring the lines between resolution and despair. One might argue it either elevates the narrative or leaves it hanging, unresolved like a thread pulled too tight.

## Discussion launchpad
*Obsession* invites fervent debate about the nature of desire and the ethical boundaries of love. Its portrayal of obsession as both a captivating and destructive force poses questions that linger uncomfortably in the mind. 

**Questions for the room:**
- Does the portrayal of obsession in this novel romanticize toxic relationships, or does it serve as a cautionary tale?
- How does Tessier’s use of an unreliable narrator affect your connection to the characters?
- In what ways does the ending challenge traditional notions of closure in dark romance?
- How does the theme of possession resonate in contemporary discussions about relationships and consent?
- What elements of craft—such as pacing and dialogue—most effectively convey the tension and obsession?
