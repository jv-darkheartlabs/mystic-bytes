## Hook & thesis
S. Massery's *Devious Obsession* is a chilling exploration of the fine line between admiration and obsession, where love becomes a dangerous game with no clear winner. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully entwines the thrill of desire with the dread of control, creating a haunting narrative that lingers long after the last page.

## The contextual pivot
In the realm of dark romance, *Devious Obsession* operates at the intersection of stalker tropes and the frenetic energy of sports romance. Massery, known for her ability to navigate complex emotional landscapes, crafts a narrative that appeals to those familiar with the genre while also inviting a broader audience. This book seamlessly bridges niche elements—obsessive love and athletic rivalry—transcending conventional boundaries. Readers drawn to the shadowy undercurrents of desire will find themselves ensnared, while newcomers to the genre may be captivated by the visceral intensity that underpins the characters’ interactions.

## Deep-dive critique
Massery’s structural choices amplify the novel’s psychological tension, utilizing a dual POV that allows readers to oscillate between the minds of both protagonist and antagonist, deepening the exploration of their motives. The pacing is deliberately unhurried, mirroring the insidious nature of obsession as it coils around the characters’ lives. Each chapter unfolds like a slow burn, punctuated by moments of acute tension where desire clashes with fear.

The prose reflects a careful balance between lyricism and rawness, with dialogues that crackle with an underlying menace—every exchange feels charged, as if the air itself is thick with unspoken words. Character development is particularly salient; the protagonist's descent into the depths of obsession is portrayed with both empathy and horror, allowing the reader to grasp the seductive allure of her fixation.

However, while the thematic ambition is commendable, certain mechanical flaws detract from the overall impact. The world-building, though vivid in its depiction of the sporting milieu, occasionally falls flat in its exploration of the emotional ramifications of the characters’ actions, leaving some thematic threads underdeveloped. The ending straddles the line between satisfying and ambiguous, leaving readers to grapple with the unresolved nature of the characters’ fates.

## Discussion launchpad
*Devious Obsession* invites discussions not only about the ethics of obsession but also about the societal implications of idolization and control within relationships. The unresolved tensions in character arcs could polarize readers—did the protagonist receive the redemption she deserved, or was her fate merely a reflection of her choices?

**Questions for the room:**
- How does the dual POV contribute to your understanding of obsession in the narrative?
- In what ways does the author blur the line between love and control, and is this effective or problematic?
- Can the portrayal of the athlete as an object of obsession resonate beyond the sports context?
- What are the implications of the ambiguous ending—does it reinforce or undermine the book’s themes?
- How does the tension created in dialogue shape your perception of the characters’ desires?
