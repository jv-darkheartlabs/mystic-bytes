## Hook & thesis
Elizabeth Helen's *Broken by Daylight* delves into the abyss of desire, exposing the collision of power and vulnerability within a world steeped in vampiric allure. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully intertwines gothic elements with a relentless exploration of brokenness, making it a hauntingly seductive addition to the dark romance genre.

## The contextual pivot
In the throes of the vampire-paranormal subgenre, Helen’s work stands as a compelling entry in the *Beasts of the Briar* series, steeped in dark fantasy tropes while also breaking new ground. The narrative layers of *Broken by Daylight* speak to an audience well-versed in both fae lore and the complexities of mortal longing, crafting a bridge between niche tropes and broader appeal. Readers familiar with Anne Rice's lush prose or the visceral tension of *A Court of Thorns and Roses* will find echoes here; yet, Helen’s nuanced exploration of trauma and resilience offers fresh perspectives that resonate well beyond the genre’s typical bounds.

## Deep-dive critique
Helen employs an intricate structure that oscillates between first-person and third-person perspectives, effectively drawing readers into the protagonist's psyche while maintaining an omniscient awareness of the surrounding chaos. This duality allows for a rich exploration of internal conflict, particularly as the protagonist grapples with her burgeoning power against the backdrop of a demanding court. 

The prose is evocative, a tapestry woven with vivid imagery that captures both the splendor and the horror of the fae realm. Descriptions of the court’s splendor juxtaposed with the bloodlust inherent in its politics create a palpable tension that thrums throughout the narrative. Dialogues crackle with subtext; every exchange feels like a game of chess, where desire and desperation dance seductively beneath the surface. 

However, pacing falters in the middle sections, where moments of introspection occasionally lag, diluting the narrative urgency. Yet, the emotional stakes are undeniably high, and the resolution is both surprising and earned, offering a cathartic release that resonates with the novel's central themes of healing through chaos. The ending, while steeped in romantic resolution, retains a haunting ambiguity that lingers long after the final page.

## Discussion launchpad
This novel raises provocative questions about the nature of desire and power dynamics within relationships, inviting a varied response among readers. The tension between vulnerability and strength creates an electric atmosphere that may polarize opinions. What remains unsaid is the deeper exploration of trauma and its manifestations in relationships, leaving gaps that challenge our understanding of consent and agency in a dark fantasy context.

- How do the themes of power and vulnerability shape your understanding of the characters’ motivations?  
- In what ways does the dual perspective enhance or detract from the storytelling experience?  
- What implications does the ending have for the characters’ future, especially regarding their traumas?  
- How does Helen's portrayal of the fae challenge or conform to traditional fantasy tropes?  
- What elements of this story evoke the most visceral reactions, and why?
