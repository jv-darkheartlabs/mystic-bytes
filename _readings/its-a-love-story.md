## Hook & thesis
Annabel Monaghan's *It's a Love Story* plunges into the intoxicating depths of forbidden desire, challenging the boundaries of love amidst societal constraints. **Verdict:** The novel deftly intertwines yearning and rebellion, positioning itself as a haunting exploration of what it means to love in the shadows.

## The contextual pivot
In the realm of forbidden love, Monaghan’s work occupies a tantalizing space that straddles both contemporary romance and darker thematic elements. Compared to titans like Anna Todd and Colleen Hoover, Monaghan crafts a narrative that appeals to those who revel in the complexities of illicit relationships while subtly inviting general readers to engage with the emotional turmoil of her characters. This novel serves not only the niche of dark romance enthusiasts but also speaks to a broader audience fascinated by the struggle between desire and morality, showcasing the author's adeptness at bridging the gap between two worlds.

## Deep-dive critique
Monaghan's narrative structure is both compelling and disorienting, employing a dual perspective that oscillates between the protagonists, allowing readers to intimately experience their conflicting desires. The pacing, while initially languid, mirrors the heat of summer's languor at the beach, gradually escalating as the stakes of their forbidden love become clearer. Dialogue crackles with unspoken tension; every interaction imbues the mundane with a sense of impending consequence. Monaghan excels at world-building, painting a vivid backdrop that feels both idyllic and suffocating, echoing the protagonists' internal struggles. However, the ending, while poignant, may feel abrupt to some, leaving threads of unresolved tension that could either frustrate or invigorate the reader’s imagination, a testament to the complexity of love itself.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel invites a rich tapestry of discussion around the nature of forbidden love and the moral dilemmas it presents. What does it mean for love to thrive in secrecy, and how does that impact our perception of its validity?

- **Questions for the room:**  
  - How does the dual POV enhance or detract from the portrayal of forbidden love?  
  - Does the beach setting serve merely as a backdrop, or does it actively influence the narrative's tension?  
  - Are the characters' choices ultimately empowering or reckless?  
  - How does Monaghan balance the thrill of desire with the weight of societal judgment?  
  - What aspects of the ending resonate most with your understanding of love's complexities?
