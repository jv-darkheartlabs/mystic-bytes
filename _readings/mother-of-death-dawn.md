## Hook & thesis
In *Mother of Death & Dawn*, Carissa Broadbent unfurls a tale steeped in sacrifice and the volatile nature of love against a backdrop of war. **Verdict:** This is a haunting exploration of how desire can be both a weapon and a balm, ultimately revealing that the fiercest battles are often fought within the heart.

## The contextual pivot
As the concluding volume of the *War of Lost Hearts* series, *Mother of Death & Dawn* sits at a compelling intersection of fantasy and romance, further solidifying Broadbent’s reputation in the genre. While it embraces familiar tropes—star-crossed lovers, a world at war, and mystical elements—it deftly subverts expectations, inviting both genre enthusiasts and general readers into a narrative rich with emotional depth and complexity. Broadbent’s work resonates with readers who appreciate the gritty textures of dark romance, akin to authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout, while also engaging those drawn to more traditional fantasy narratives.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Mother of Death & Dawn* is both its strength and its challenge. By employing a non-linear timeline, Broadbent crafts a tapestry of interwoven pasts and presents that enhance the emotional stakes. The dual perspectives of the main characters, rife with longing and betrayal, amplify the tension inherent in their tumultuous relationship. Dialogue sparkles with an undercurrent of desire, often layered with double meanings that enrich character interactions. However, this complexity can occasionally lead to pacing issues; moments of introspection may linger longer than necessary, potentially undermining the urgency of the central conflict.

Thematically, Broadbent excels in portraying the ambivalence of love amidst chaos. The motif of the 'flaming sword' serves as a potent symbol of both destruction and protection, encapsulating the dual nature of the characters' desires. World-building is immersive, with evocative imagery that paints a vivid picture of the landscape ravaged by war, yet moments of exposition can feel heavy-handed, risking the fluidity of the narrative. The ending, while satisfying, may polarize readers; it is earned, but the resolution might feel like a bittersweet victory—offering closure while leaving echoes of unresolved emotional turmoil.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel’s exploration of desire and conflict invites rich discussions on the nature of love in extreme circumstances, where the lines between heroism and selfishness blur. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the duality of love presented in this book reflect on real-world relationships?  
- In what ways does the non-linear timeline enhance or detract from the story's emotional impact?  
- What did you think of the symbolism of the 'flaming sword' in relation to love and sacrifice?  
- How do Broadbent’s choices in pacing and structure affect your engagement with the characters?  
- Does the bittersweet nature of the ending work for you, or would you have preferred a different resolution?
