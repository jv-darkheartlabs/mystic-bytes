## Hook & thesis
Molly Doyle's *Scream For Us* plunges readers into a haunting exploration of desire and obsession, where the boundaries of love and madness blur within the shadowy embrace of a secret society. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully intertwines gothic horror and romance, creating a chilling landscape where the heart's darkest yearnings are laid bare.

## The contextual pivot
In the realm of gothic horror-romance, Doyle's work carves out a niche that melds psychological tension with supernatural allure, reminiscent of authors like Sarah Waters and Silvia Moreno-Garcia. The *Order of the Unseen* serves as a compelling backdrop, drawing readers into a world where cult themes and violence are not merely plot devices but reflections of the characters' internal struggles. This book deftly bridges niche tropes and broader themes, appealing to both avid fans of dark romance and those seeking a deeper examination of obsession and human frailty.

## Deep-dive critique
Doyle's craftsmanship shines through her intricate structure and character development. The alternating perspectives create a layered narrative that invites readers to witness the characters' spirals into obsession, enhancing the tension throughout the story. The pacing, however, occasionally falters, leading to moments where the narrative feels drawn out, causing the reader's engagement to wane slightly. 

The prose is rich and evocative, painting vivid imagery that immerses readers in the gothic atmosphere, yet at times, it teeters on the edge of overwhelming, risking clarity for style. Characters are well-developed; their desires and fears resonate deeply, particularly the protagonist, whose internal conflicts are both relatable and unsettling. However, some secondary characters feel underexplored, leaving questions about their motivations and roles in the overarching narrative.

Doyle does not shy away from graphic content, using visceral imagery to evoke a sense of dread that complements the romantic tension. Yet, this approach may alienate some readers, as the graphic violence juxtaposes sharply with tender moments, challenging conventional expectations of romance. The ending, while striking, raises questions about the morality of the characters' choices, leaving it open to interpretation rather than providing a tidy resolution, which may polarize readers.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of desire and horror in *Scream For Us* invites a rich discussion on the ethics of obsession and the nature of love within a gothic framework. How Doyle navigates these themes—balancing romantic allure with psychological terror—will likely elicit varied responses.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the graphic nature of the violence enhance or detract from the romantic elements?  
- How effectively does Doyle portray the internal struggles of her characters, and does this affect your empathy for them?  
- In what ways does the secret society function as a character in its own right?  
- How does this work compare to other gothic romances you’ve read in terms of thematic exploration?  
- What unresolved questions linger for you at the conclusion of the novel?
