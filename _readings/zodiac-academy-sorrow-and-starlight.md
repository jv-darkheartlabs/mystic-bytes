## Hook & thesis
In *Zodiac Academy: Sorrow and Starlight*, Caroline Peckham and Susanne Valenti delve into the turbulent waters of identity and power dynamics, cloaked within the familiar tropes of bully romance. **Verdict:** This book flirts dangerously with the edges of desire and trauma, ultimately revealing that the true conflict lies in the characters' struggle for agency amidst manipulation and grief.

## The contextual pivot
Situated within the sprawling universe of bully romance, *Sorrow and Starlight* stands out amidst its contemporaries, such as *Cruel Intentions* and the works of Penelope Douglas, by weaving in elements of fantasy that mirror the complexities of high school hierarchies and interpersonal cruelty. Peckham and Valenti's collaboration has established a niche that resonates with readers craving both emotional depth and the thrill of forbidden attraction. The invisible audience here spans young adults and seasoned romance aficionados, bridging dark thematic elements with a seductive narrative that invites both escapism and introspection.

## Deep-dive critique
The book's structure employs a dual POV, effectively allowing readers to inhabit both the protagonist's psyche and the antagonistic forces in her life. This choice amplifies the tension, enabling a rich exploration of the push-and-pull dynamic that defines the relationships at Zodiac Academy. Peckham and Valenti adeptly utilize pacing to reflect the characters' emotional states—moments of breathless action are juxtaposed with quieter, introspective passages that delve into the characters' profound grief and unresolved traumas. 

The prose is a sharp tool, enhancing the gothic romanticism that permeates the narrative. Character dialogue crackles with intensity, capturing the ferocity of young love and the bitterness of betrayal. However, while the authors excel in crafting vivid, visceral interactions, some world-building elements feel underdeveloped, leaving readers yearning for a more fleshed-out understanding of the academy's lore and its impact on the characters' motivations. The thematic exploration of death and violence serves as a potent backdrop, yet at times threatens to overshadow the romantic tension that the genre promises. The ending, while climactic, feels a touch rushed—a bittersweet resolution that leaves a lingering sense of unsettled desire and unanswered questions.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel's interplay of power, trauma, and romance invites a rich discourse on the nature of desire in toxic relationships and the implications of grief on personal agency. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the dual POV shape our perception of morality within the story?  
- Are the romantic relationships built on a foundation of trauma and manipulation ultimately redeemable?  
- What does the portrayal of grief suggest about personal growth in the face of adversity?  
- Where does the balance lie between dark themes and romantic escapism in bully romance?  
- In what ways might the book's unresolved elements reflect the complexities of real-life relationships?
