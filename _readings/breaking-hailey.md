## Hook & thesis
In *Breaking Hailee*, I. A. Dice crafts a haunting exploration of obsession that delves into the dark recesses of the human psyche. **Verdict:** This book masterfully entwines desire and danger, serving as a chilling reminder of how love can morph into a consuming fixation.

## The contextual pivot
*Breaking Hailee* occupies a precarious space within the stalker-obsession subgenre, reminiscent of works by authors like Tarryn Fisher and Anna Zaires, yet it stands on its own with a distinctive voice. Dice’s narrative is both a traditional gothic romance and a psychological thriller, blending niche tropes of obsession and manipulation while appealing to a broader audience fascinated by the darker aspects of love. The invisible audience here seems to straddle between those who revel in dark romance and readers exploring psychological depth—each layer offering a more complex and unsettling portrait of human connection.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Breaking Hailee* is a delicate web, with pacing that oscillates between breathless tension and moments of suffocating stillness. The dual perspectives provide insight into both the stalker’s twisted motivations and Hailee’s spiraling fear, yet at times, this switch can jolt the reader out of the immersive experience. Dice's prose is striking, with vivid imagery that evokes the claustrophobic atmosphere dwelling in both Hailee's mind and her surroundings. However, the dialogue occasionally veers into cliché, which detracts from the overall authenticity of the characters’ emotional struggles. 

Thematically, the novel grapples with the concept of identity within obsession, as Hailee’s sense of self becomes increasingly distorted by her captor’s influence. The visceral world-building reinforces this theme; the oppressive settings mirror emotional turmoil, making the reader acutely aware of the protagonist's vulnerability. The ending, while suspenseful, does raise questions about the resolution of character arcs—does Hailee truly escape her torment, or is she merely stepping into a new phase of victimhood? Such ambiguities reflect Dice’s intention to unsettle rather than provide tidy conclusions, aligning the narrative with the complexities of obsession itself.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay between love and madness creates polarizing reactions in readers, prompting intense debates about morality and emotional boundaries. 

**Questions for the room:**
- How effectively does Dice balance the line between romantic longing and psychological manipulation?
- In what ways does the dual POV enhance or detract from our understanding of obsession?
- Do we sympathize with Hailee, or does her character become too passive in the face of her circumstances?
- How does the narrative challenge traditional notions of agency in relationships?
- What are the ethical implications of portraying such obsessive love in literature?
