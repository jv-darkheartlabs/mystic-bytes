## Hook & thesis
In Mia Sheridan's *More Than Words*, the author deftly explores the intricate dynamics of love, loss, and the barriers that fracture human connection. **Verdict:** Sheridan's narrative dismantles the trope of forbidden love to reveal a profound exploration of vulnerability and communication that transcends spoken language.

## The contextual pivot
As a notable entry in the forbidden-love genre, *More Than Words* occupies a unique space where disability and the complexities of grief intertwine with romance. Sheridan's previous works often delve into the darker aspects of love, yet this novel bridges niche tropes of dark romance with broader themes of emotional resonance, making it accessible to a wider audience. The invisible readers—those drawn to stories that challenge societal norms—will find themselves captivated by the layered portrayals of intimacy and isolation.

## Deep-dive critique
Sheridan employs a dual perspective that alternates between the protagonists, allowing readers to intimately experience their insecurities and desires. This structure not only builds tension but also deepens emotional investment, particularly in the way silence is laden with meaning. The use of sign language as a motif is a brilliant choice, as it symbolizes the barriers not just of communication but of personal trauma. 

The prose is lyrical, punctuated by moments of raw honesty that evoke a visceral response. Sheridan’s portrayal of grief is particularly poignant; it does not merely linger in the background but shapes the characters' decisions and their interactions. However, at times, the pacing falters, particularly in the latter sections where the resolution feels rushed. The climax, while intended to be cathartic, risks undermining the complexities established earlier in the narrative, leaving the reader yearning for a more intricate unraveling of the characters' fates. 

Despite these mechanical flaws, the thematic exploration of how love can emerge from the ashes of trauma and the power dynamics inherent in relationships is masterfully rendered. The ending, although somewhat tidy, manages to encapsulate the essence of resilience and hope amidst heartbreak.

## Discussion launchpad
The narrative presents several polarities that could ignite a rich dialogue within the group. 
- The tension between the need for independence and the desire for connection serves as a profound backdrop to the characters' journeys.
- The portrayal of disability and how it shapes interpersonal relationships raises questions about representation in romance.
- The pacing towards the end invites debate on the necessity of a conventional resolution in stories that delve into complex emotional landscapes.

- In what ways does Sheridan challenge or conform to typical forbidden-love tropes?  
- Is the rushed conclusion a disservice to the characters’ development, or does it serve a greater thematic purpose?  
- How does the exploration of grief influence your perception of the characters’ actions and choices?  
- What are the implications of representing disability in a romance narrative?
