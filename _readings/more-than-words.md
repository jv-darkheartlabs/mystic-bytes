## Hook & thesis
*More Than Words* by Mia Sheridan invites readers into a world where silence becomes a canvas for forbidden love, a narrative steeped in yearning and unspoken bonds. **Verdict:** This novel captures the exquisite pain of longing through the delicate art of sign language, elevating it beyond mere tropes of romance.

## The contextual pivot
In the landscape of forbidden love narratives, Sheridan's work resonates with a distinct authenticity, merging the tactile intimacy of sign language with the emotional gravitas of hidden desires. Her exploration of love that transcends spoken words echoes the thematic richness found in titles like *The Hating Game* and *Beautiful Disaster*, yet here, the stakes are heightened by silence. Sheridan's previous works often delve into the complexities of love laced with emotional scars, making this novel a compelling addition to her oeuvre. It deftly bridges niche interests—such as communication barriers and the intricacies of sign language—to a broader audience, inviting both seasoned dark romance enthusiasts and newcomers alike to experience its captivating depth.

## Deep-dive critique
Sheridan skillfully employs a nonlinear structure, weaving past and present threads that enhance character development and emotional resonance. The dual POV enriches the narrative, allowing readers to experience the internal struggles of both protagonists, which serves to heighten empathy and investment in their journey. The pacing, while at times languid, carefully aligns with the characters' emotional states—each lingering moment of connection is drawn out like a whisper, generating tension that vibrates beneath the surface. 

Dialogue, or the lack thereof, is particularly noteworthy; the use of sign language becomes a powerful tool, not only for communication but also for building a palpable sense of intimacy and urgency. Sheridan's prose is lush yet precise, painting vivid imagery that brings the characters' experiences to life—each gesture imbued with meaning, each touch electrifying the air between them. However, some readers may find the exploration of silence a double-edged sword, where moments of tension occasionally teeter on the brink of repetitiveness. The ending, though emotionally charged, could polarize opinions: is it a satisfying resolution or a bittersweet evocation of what remains unfulfilled?

## Discussion launchpad
The complex layers of *More Than Words* invite rich discussions about the nature of communication, desire, and the barriers that love must overcome. Yet, lurking beneath the surface are tensions—how effectively does silence communicate? Are we drawn in by the longing, or does it frustrate?

- Does the ending feel earned, or does it leave too many questions unanswered?
