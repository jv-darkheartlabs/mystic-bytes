## Hook & thesis
Cassandra Clare's *Clockwork Angel* deftly intertwines steampunk elements with dark romance to unearth the complexities of love and betrayal amidst a backdrop of supernatural conflict. **Verdict:** This novel expertly navigates the gears of desire, making it a compelling entry in the fantasy-romance genre.

## The contextual pivot
*Clockwork Angel* sits comfortably within the steampunk and dark romance subgenres, emerging as the first installment of *The Infernal Devices* trilogy. Clare builds upon the vibrant landscape of Victorian London, merging historical elements with fantastical creatures and shadowy forces, which positions this work alongside other novels like Philip Pullman's *His Dark Materials* and the works of Gail Carriger. The invisible audience here is likely drawn from both established fans of Clare’s *Shadowhunters* series and newcomers to the genre; it navigates the niche allure of supernatural romance while remaining accessible to a broader readership through its rich world-building and relatable emotional stakes.

## Deep-dive critique
The novel's structure is carefully crafted, utilizing a third-person limited point of view that allows readers to intimately experience Tessa Gray's journey. Clare's pacing is deliberate, balancing moments of tension with more reflective passages that deepen character development. This choice effectively enhances the thematic core of transformation—both personal and mechanical—as Tessa grapples with her identity and abilities in a world that oscillates between beauty and monstrosity. 

Dialogue sparkles with wit, echoing the social intricacies of a Victorian setting, while also conveying the longing and tension that underscores the romantic entanglements. Clare's prose is lush and evocative, immersing readers in the atmospheric details of a world where clockwork angels coexist with human emotion. However, the narrative occasionally succumbs to predictability, especially in romantic arcs that may feel familiar to genre enthusiasts. The ending, while satisfying, teeters on the edge of cliché, leaving some narrative threads tantalizingly unresolved yet ultimately feeling earned within the context of character growth.

## Discussion launchpad
*Clockwork Angel* raises significant questions about the nature of agency and desire within its fantastical framework. The interplay between the steampunk aesthetic and the darker elements of romance invites readers to explore the boundaries of love and loyalty in a world filled with treachery and enchantment. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Clare’s portrayal of Tessa challenge or reinforce stereotypes of female agency in romance?  
- In what ways does the steampunk setting enhance or detract from the romantic elements of the story?  
- Does the predictability of certain tropes weaken the overall impact of the narrative, or do they serve a purpose in the romantic and gothic tradition?  
- How do the themes of transformation resonate with contemporary societal issues regarding identity and belonging?  
- What unresolved tensions in the plot or character arcs linger after the final page?
