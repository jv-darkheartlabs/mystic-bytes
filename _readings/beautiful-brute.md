## Hook & thesis
*Beautiful Brute* by Eden O'Neill is a visceral exploration of desire and domination, where beauty conceals brutality beneath its surface. **Verdict:** This book succeeds in its strikingly gothic portrayal of the complex interplay between power and attraction, making it an unsettling yet addictive read.

## The contextual pivot
Situated firmly within the bully-romance subgenre, *Beautiful Brute* draws on classic elements of gothic literature and contemporary dark romance. O'Neill crafts a narrative that feels both familiar and fresh, appealing to readers who revel in the tension of forbidden love and the psychological games that define it. This work continues the trajectory established in the previous titles of the Court University series, where emotional manipulation and alluring danger are the hallmarks of romantic entanglements. The invisible audience oscillates between devoted fans of dark romance and those curious about the allure of the bully trope, effectively bridging niche themes to broader readership.

## Deep-dive critique
O'Neill's writing shines through its rich, evocative prose, which serves to heighten both the aesthetic beauty and the underlying violence of the characters' interactions. The structure of the narrative, alternating between the protagonist's internal conflicts and the external chaos brought forth by her love interest, creates a compelling rhythm that draws readers deeper into the emotional labyrinth. 

The first-person point of view allows readers an intimate glimpse into the protagonist's psyche, rendering her desires palpable while also revealing her vulnerabilities. This choice amplifies the tension between her yearning for connection and the perilous nature of her relationship with the titular brute. However, at times, the pacing falters, particularly in the midsection where repetitive character dynamics risk diluting the intensity of their exchanges. Yet, O'Neill redeems herself in the climax, where the stakes are raised, resulting in an ending that feels both earned and painfully inevitable. Themes of beauty intertwined with cruelty are explored with a deft hand, presenting moral complexities that leave a lingering echo long after the last page.

## Discussion launchpad
The emotional high-wire act O'Neill performs invites debate on the moral implications of romanticizing toxic relationships. Are the characters' actions justified by their desires, or do they reflect a concerning trend in how we view love? Moreover, the book's gothic elements challenge readers to confront the darkness within beauty, provoking questions about societal standards and personal worth.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does O'Neill's portrayal of beauty and brutality reflect societal norms?  
- Can we separate the characters' toxic traits from their allure, or does one inherently diminish the other?  
- In what ways does the pacing affect your investment in the characters?  
- How does the first-person POV influence your understanding of desire and power dynamics in the narrative?  
- What unresolved tensions linger for you after reaching the conclusion?
