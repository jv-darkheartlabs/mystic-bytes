## Hook & thesis
In *Every Summer After*, Carley Fortune weaves a poignant tale of love and loss that transcends the conventions of the forbidden-love genre. The book's true engine is the struggle between the desire for connection and the weight of past grievances. **Verdict:** Fortune's exploration of nostalgia and trauma crafts a deeply resonant narrative that lingers long after the last page.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the realm of forbidden love, *Every Summer After* treads familiar ground, yet Fortune breathes new life into the trope by embedding it within the complexities of grief and addiction. Readers familiar with the works of Emily Henry or Taylor Jenkins Reid may find echoes of their stylistic choices here, with an emphasis on emotional vulnerability. However, Fortune’s narrative bridges niche tropes to a broader audience through its universal themes of second chances and the haunting presence of regret, making it accessible even to those who might typically shy away from darker romantic themes.

## Deep-dive critique
Fortune's craft shines in her deft handling of dual timelines, which effectively juxtaposes the innocence of youthful love against the somber realities of adulthood. The nonlinear structure allows readers to gradually uncover the layers of heartbreak and longing that permeate the narrative. The pacing, particularly in the early chapters, may feel languid to some; however, this serves to mirror the protagonist's nostalgic recollections, immersing the reader in the weight of memory.

Characterization is another strong suit, with the protagonist’s internal conflict rendered through rich, evocative prose. Fortune skillfully employs dialogue that crackles with unspoken tension, encapsulating the fraught dynamics of rekindled relationships. Yet, the narrative occasionally falls prey to melodrama, particularly in its resolution; some may find the ending a touch too neatly tied, lacking the raw unpredictability that characterizes real-life relationships.

The thematic exploration of grief and addiction is particularly poignant, serving as a backdrop to the romantic entanglement and adding depth to the characters’ motivations. Fortune does not shy away from depicting the darker aspects of love, providing a counterbalance to the lighter moments that often define summer romances. This duality enhances the emotional stakes and invites readers to reflect on the complexities of human connection.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of nostalgia and regret in *Every Summer After* invites varying interpretations, particularly regarding how we reconcile past relationships with present realities. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do you feel the dual timelines impact your understanding of the characters’ motivations?  
- In what ways does Fortune challenge or reinforce traditional tropes of forbidden love?  
- Do you believe the ending was earned, or did it undermine the story's emotional weight?  
- How does the portrayal of addiction shape your perception of the romantic elements within the narrative?  
- What role does grief play in shaping the characters' decisions, and how does it resonate with your own experiences of love?
