## Hook & thesis
In *Every Summer After*, Carley Fortune deftly navigates the turbulent waters of forbidden love and second chances, revealing that the heart's true longing often lies buried beneath layers of regret and nostalgia. **Verdict:** This novel serves as a haunting ode to summer's ephemeral magic and the shadows it casts on enduring relationships.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the realm of dark romance, *Every Summer After* flirts with the conventions of the second-chance narrative, echoing the emotional heft found in works like *The Night Circus* and *Beach Read*. Fortune’s portrayal of youthful fervor and the inevitable passage of time resonates with readers who relish the tension of rekindled passions, while also appealing to a broader audience through its accessible prose and vivid imagery. This book treads the line between niche and mainstream, drawing in those captivated by the complexities of love that is both forbidden and familiar.

## Deep-dive critique
Fortune’s crafting of *Every Summer After* is marked by a poignant structure that alternates between past and present, skillfully revealing the evolution of desire. This dual timeline not only enriches the character development but also enhances the thematic exploration of memory’s bittersweet nature. The pacing ebbs and flows, echoing the rhythm of summer itself, creating a sense of urgency as the protagonists grapple with their unresolved feelings. 

Dialogue in the novel is particularly striking; it encapsulates the raw vulnerability of the characters, pulling readers into their emotional turmoil. For instance, the exchanges between the central figures are laden with a mixture of joy and sorrow, effectively capturing the essence of their complicated history. However, while the prose is lush and evocative, certain moments risk overwrought sentimentality, potentially alienating those who prefer a more restrained expression of yearning. 

The conclusion, while satisfying, raises questions about the nature of closure and the choices that lead us back to love, leaving the reader with a potent blend of hope and uncertainty. This ambivalence invites deeper reflection on whether true reconciliation is ever fully realized.

## Discussion launchpad
As *Every Summer After* plunges into the depths of love and loss, it presents polarities that may ignite debate among readers. The tension between nostalgia and the desire for new beginnings could split opinions, as could the concept of destiny versus choice in relationships.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the dual timeline enhance or detract from the emotional stakes of the narrative?  
- In what ways do the characters embody the themes of regret and redemption?  
- Does the ending provide a satisfying resolution, or does it leave too many questions unanswered?  
- How effectively does Fortune balance the elements of forbidden love with the broader context of personal growth?  
- What role does the setting play in shaping the characters’ experiences and desires?
