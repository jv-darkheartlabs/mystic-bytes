## Hook & thesis
In "Consider Me," Becka Mack masterfully weaves a tale of tumultuous passion and bitter rivalry, where the lines between love and hate blur in the most intoxicating of ways. **Verdict:** This novel is a compelling exploration of desire that grips the reader and refuses to let go, even as it teeters on the edge of darkness.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled firmly within the enemies-to-lovers trope, Mack's work carves a niche in contemporary romance while resonating with the gothic elements of tension and emotional strife. Unlike traditional romance novels that often glide over conflict, Mack immerses her characters in an emotional landscape fraught with misunderstandings and simmering attraction, akin to the works of authors like Talia Hibbert and Christina Lauren, who also navigate the complexities of love through sharp wit and layered conflict. This book speaks not only to die-hard fans of dark romance but also bridges toward a broader audience, inviting general readers into the electrifying chaos of its character dynamics.

## Deep-dive critique
Mack's narrative structure expertly manipulates pacing, shifting seamlessly between simmering confrontations and explosive moments that heighten the stakes of the protagonists' relationship. The alternating points of view provide an intimate glimpse into the inner turmoil each character faces, making their eventual connection feel both inevitable and fraught with tension. The dialogue is laced with sharp humor that often undercuts the gravity of their situations, enhancing the emotional stakes without sacrificing the story's momentum.

However, while the prose shines with vivid imagery and compelling characterizations, some thematic elements—particularly concerning the darker aspects of the characters' pasts—could benefit from deeper exploration. The resolution feels slightly rushed, leaving some threads dangling, yet it remains emotionally resonant, capturing the essence of love that emerges from chaos. The ending, while not entirely predictable, feels earned due to the characters' growth and the tumultuous journey they undertake.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel's juxtaposition of love and animosity provides fertile ground for debate, as readers may find themselves polarized by the characters' choices and the implications of their relationships.

**Questions for the room:**
- How effectively do you think Mack navigates the line between romantic tension and unhealthy dynamics?
- What role does humor play in balancing the darker themes of the story?
- Do you believe the ending reconciles the characters' journeys, or does it leave too many questions unanswered?
- In what ways does the book challenge or reinforce traditional tropes in the enemies-to-lovers genre?
- How does the portrayal of desire influence your perception of the characters' development throughout the narrative?
