## Hook & thesis
*Consider Me* by Becka Mack emerges as an electrifying exploration of the enemies-to-lovers trope, where sharp wit collides with vulnerability to reveal deeper truths about trust and emotional intimacy. **Verdict:** It deftly navigates the turbulent waters of animosity and desire, ultimately illustrating that love can often wear the guise of conflict.

## The contextual pivot
In the current landscape of contemporary romance, *Consider Me* dances within the whimsical yet dark shadows of the enemies-to-lovers genre. Mack’s narrative sits comfortably alongside authors like Tessa Bailey and Christina Lauren, who masterfully blend humor with emotional depth. This novel caters to a niche audience that appreciates a cocktail of sharp banter and raw tension, yet opens its arms to general readers through its accessible humor and relatable themes. The invisible audience is one that revels in the juxtaposition of laughter and heartbreak, making it a bridge between niche dark romance enthusiasts and broader romantic comedy fans.

## Deep-dive critique
Mack’s structural choices, particularly in pacing and point of view (POV), amplify the narrative’s emotional stakes. The alternating perspectives between the two protagonists invite readers into their internal conflicts, shaping a dual lens through which animosity transforms into affection. The dialogue crackles with tension, as each exchange is layered with unspoken desires, revealing the characters' vulnerabilities beneath their antagonistic facades. 

However, while the prose is infused with humor, moments of melodrama occasionally disrupt the flow, risking the thrilling momentum that the narrative builds. The thematic exploration of fear and vulnerability in relationships is compelling, especially as the characters grapple with their pasts. Yet, certain plot devices, such as misunderstandings or timed revelations, can feel contrived, at times weakening the story's authenticity. The ending, while satisfying in its resolution of conflict, raises questions about the depth of character growth, leaving the reader yearning for a more profound transformation. 

## Discussion launchpad
Mack’s work straddles the line between light-hearted romance and the darker implications of emotional conflict, which may provoke divided opinions on the effectiveness of humor in serious contexts. The juxtaposition of laugh-out-loud moments with emotional nuance raises questions about the balance between levity and gravity in romantic narratives.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does humor affect the portrayal of darker themes in romance?  
- Do you find the characters' development believable, or does it fall into cliché?  
- In what ways does the dual POV enhance or detract from the enemies-to-lovers experience?  
- How do you interpret the ending — is it an authentic resolution or a convenient wrap-up?  
- What role does banter play in establishing the tension and desire between characters?
