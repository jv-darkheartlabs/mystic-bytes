## Hook & thesis
In *That Kind of Guy*, Stephanie Archer deftly navigates the treacherous waters of enemies-to-lovers and fake dating, exposing the raw, visceral tension that lies within confrontational intimacy. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully intertwines conflict and longing, creating a narrative that challenges the boundaries of desire and animosity.

## The contextual pivot
Archer's work finds its niche within the saturated landscape of dark romance, specifically the enemies-to-lovers trope, where animosity often masks a deeper yearning. This novel stands as a compelling exploration of duality, reminiscent of the sharp, intense dynamics found in works by authors like Talia Hibbert and S.L. Jennings. Archer’s ability to bridge niche themes—sharp repartee and the undercurrents of vulnerability—speaks to a readership that craves complexity in romance, drawing in both seasoned dark romance aficionados and those more casually exploring the genre.

## Deep-dive critique
Structurally, *That Kind of Guy* unfolds with a confident pacing that echoes the tension simmering between its protagonists. The dual points of view provide an intimate glimpse into the characters' conflicting emotions, allowing readers to grasp the intricacies of their mutual disdain and undeniable attraction. Archer's prose is laced with sharp dialogue that crackles with tension and wit, effectively illustrating the push-pull dynamic that lies at the heart of the story. 

However, the narrative occasionally falters in its pacing; certain moments of introspection feel prolonged, risking the momentum built through earlier confrontations. Characterization shines through the well-constructed arcs of both leads, who are fleshed out with vulnerabilities that render them relatable, yet impeccably flawed. Archer's world-building, set against the backdrop of Queen's Cove, is vivid but sometimes leans too heavily on cliché; the setting could have further embraced its gothic undertones to enhance the overall atmosphere of desire and danger. While the ending provides resolution, it teeters on the edge of predictability, potentially leaving readers craving a more daring conclusion that embraces the complexities presented throughout the narrative.

## Discussion launchpad
*That Kind of Guy* presents a rich tapestry of conflict and desire, yet it also leaves room for debate about the effectiveness of its resolution and the balance of genre expectations. The novel's exploration of themes such as vulnerability and power dynamics invites a deeper analysis of character motivations and reader biases.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Do you find the enemies-to-lovers trope more compelling when it challenges societal norms or when it adheres to them?  
- How does the dual POV affect your investment in the central conflict?  
- In what ways does the setting amplify or detract from the emotional stakes?  
- What are the implications of using a predictable ending in a genre that thrives on tension and unpredictability?  
- How does Archer’s handling of humor within tension influence your perception of the characters' relationship?
