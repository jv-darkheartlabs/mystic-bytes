## Hook & thesis
In "The Love of My Afterlife," Kirsty Greenwood delves into the perilous territory of forbidden love, where desire transcends the boundaries of life and death. **Verdict:** This haunting exploration of love's tenacity unveils the raw, unyielding nature of human connection, even when it dances with the macabre.

## The contextual pivot
Rooted firmly in the forbidden-love genre, Greenwood's narrative intertwines elements of gothic romance with the spectral whispers of the afterlife. While similar in tone to the works of authors like Sarah Waters and Anne Rice, Greenwood's exploration of the ethereal offers a fresh perspective, appealing to both niche enthusiasts and general readers. The palpable tension between the lovers—one rooted in the physical world and the other shrouded in the afterlife—creates a rich tapestry of longing that resonates across audiences, bridging traditional tropes with contemporary sensibilities.

## Deep-dive critique
The novel thrives on its intricate structure, employing alternating points of view that enhance the emotional stakes between the living and the dead. This technique allows readers to experience the protagonist's internal struggle as she grapples with her profound attraction to a spectral figure. Greenwood's prose flows with lyrical elegance, imbuing the narrative with a sense of urgency that mirrors the characters' racing hearts. The dialogue, infused with a blend of yearning and despair, serves as a catalyst for the reader’s empathetic engagement, drawing us into the depths of their tumultuous desires.

However, while the pacing is generally effective, moments of exposition occasionally disrupt the hypnotic rhythm, creating a slight imbalance in tension. The thematic exploration of love as a force that defies even death is compelling, yet at times feels undercooked in its resolution. The ending, while poignant, leaves lingering questions about the implications of their love—a choice that could either be viewed as brave or frustratingly vague.

## Discussion launchpad
This novel’s exploration of love that transcends mortality invites varied interpretations, particularly concerning the ethical implications of such unions and the nature of desire itself.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the portrayal of desire in a forbidden context affect our perception of the characters' morality?  
- Does the supernatural element enhance or detract from the emotional depth of the relationship?  
- In what ways does the novel challenge or reinforce traditional views on love and loss?  
- How do you perceive the balance between narrative tension and thematic resolution in the ending?  
- What role does the setting play in shaping the characters’ experiences and desires?
