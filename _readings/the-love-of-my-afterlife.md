## Hook & thesis
*The Love of My Afterlife* by Kirsty Greenwood is a daring exploration of the intersection between yearning and duty, wrapped in a forbidden romance that transcends mortal bounds. **Verdict:** This novel deftly unravels the complexities of love entangled with loss, ultimately revealing the haunting power of desire that lingers even in the afterlife.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the forbidden love trope, Greenwood’s latest offering delves into the gothic nuances of romance intertwined with life and death. It calls to mind works such as *The Lovely Bones* by Alice Sebold, where ethereal realms meet human emotion. Yet, Greenwood carves a unique path, blending the lightheartedness of rom-com elements with a darker, more poignant undercurrent. This book speaks to an audience yearning for depth in their escapism, transcending niche boundaries to connect with those who seek the thrill of taboo love.

## Deep-dive critique
Structurally, Greenwood employs a dual perspective that juxtaposes the living and the afterlife, effectively crafting a layered narrative that invites the reader to navigate between the vibrancy of life and the somberness of death. The pacing, particularly in the middle sections, occasionally stumbles, as the emotional weight of the protagonist's choices can feel drawn out, yet it serves to heighten desire and anticipation. Character development shines through nuanced dialogue, where the protagonist's inner conflict oscillates between longing and the moral implications of her choices. This tension is palpable, creating an atmosphere saturated with yearning. World-building is rife with imaginative details that blur the lines of reality, though some may find the fantastical elements teeter on implausibility. The ending, while bittersweet, feels earned, encapsulating the essence of love that endures beyond the veil, leaving readers with a haunting resonance.

## Discussion launchpad
As we delve into the complexities of this narrative, polarizing themes emerge between the nature of love and the ethics of desire after death. The interplay of humor and tragedy may also spur debate on the appropriate blending of tones in such a weighty subject. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the dual perspective enhance or complicate the narrative?  
- In what ways does the book challenge conventional ideas of love?  
- Are the fantastical elements a strength or a distraction from the core message?  
- How do humor and darkness coexist in the portrayal of forbidden love?  
- What lingering questions do you have about the choices made by the protagonist?
