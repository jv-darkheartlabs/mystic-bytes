## Hook & thesis
In *Carnival Stalker*, Selena Winters plunges readers into a world where obsession becomes a carnival ride of both thrill and terror. **Verdict:** This novel excels in its exploration of the psychological depths of desire, transforming the trope of the stalker into a complex, unsettling character study that leaves one questioning the boundaries of love and possession.

## The contextual pivot
Situated within the stalker-obsession genre, *Carnival Stalker* intertwines the fantastical allure of carnival life with the darker sides of human connection. Winters, known for her ability to dissect the nuances of desire, presents a narrative that both adheres to and subverts genre expectations. The invisible audience here seems to be readers who favor dark romance but crave a narrative that doesn’t shy away from the complexities and ethical quandaries of obsession. Comparatively, authors like Tarryn Fisher and Anna Zaires have tread similar paths, yet Winters’ distinctive blending of whimsy with dread sets her work apart, enticing both niche aficionados and general readers to grapple with its provocative themes.

## Deep-dive critique
Winters’ craft demonstrates a deft command of pacing that mirrors the dizzying highs and lows of the protagonist’s emotional state. The alternating timelines create a tension that propels the narrative forward, keeping the reader on edge as they uncover layers of the stalker's psyche. The first-person POV draws readers intimately into the protagonist's thoughts, allowing them to experience the intoxicating blend of fear and desire that fuels the obsession. Dialogues are sharp and loaded with subtext, effectively illustrating how manipulation can masquerade as affection in the throes of obsession. 

However, the prose occasionally stumbles with overly melodramatic moments that threaten to undermine the psychological authenticity. While the carnival setting is richly described, with vivid imagery that evokes both beauty and decay, some elements risk veering into cliché, potentially alienating readers seeking genuine psychological depth. The ending, while climactic, may leave some feeling unresolved, as it grapples with the consequences of obsession rather than offering a neatly tied-up resolution. This thematic choice, though bold, may polarize readers, raising questions about the nature of redemption in relationships fraught with danger.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel’s exploration of obsession versus love presents a fertile ground for debate, particularly as readers may find themselves torn between empathy for the characters and repulsion for their actions. The dichotomy between romanticizing dark themes and the responsibility of representation is particularly relevant here.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the portrayal of the stalker challenge or reinforce existing stereotypes in dark romance?  
- In what ways does the carnival setting enhance or detract from the narrative’s themes of obsession?  
- Can we truly empathize with characters who engage in possessive behavior, or does that complicate our understanding of love?  
- What are the ethical boundaries in writing romance that explores themes of obsession and possessiveness?  
- How did the ending shape your perception of the characters and their journey?
