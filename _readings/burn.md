## Hook & thesis
*Burn* by Clarissa Wild ignites a ferocious exploration of obsession cloaked in the fiery guise of enemies-to-lovers. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully dissects the chaotic dance of desire and revenge, compelling readers to confront the darker corners of passion.

## The contextual pivot
In the crowded landscape of dark romance, *Burn* occupies a niche that plays with the intensity of rivalry and the subsequent combustion of attraction. Wild, known for her deft handling of complex emotional landscapes, bridges the gap between specialized tropes and mainstream appeal. Fans of authors like Ana Huang and Colleen Hoover might find this work a tantalizing exploration of the line between love and hate, while newcomers can revel in its raw, visceral emotionality.

## Deep-dive critique
Wild’s structural choices amplify the emotional stakes, deploying a first-person dual POV that plunges readers into the jagged edges of both protagonists’ psyches. This technique not only builds intimacy but also accentuates the conflict — each character’s motivations are laid bare, and the tension between them simmers with an undercurrent of unresolved animosity. The pacing, frenetic yet precise, draws out key moments of both confrontation and connection, pushing the narrative to a fever pitch that mirrors the characters’ tumultuous relationship.

The prose is rich, laden with metaphor that evokes the senses; the imagery of fire and ash illuminates the theme of destruction as a precursor to rebirth. However, occasional lapses into melodrama can detract from the otherwise sharp dialogue, drawing attention away from the higher stakes at play. The world-building, while not as expansive as in some contemporaries, remains effective, capturing the pulse of an urban setting that heightens the intimacy of the characters’ encounters.

The denouement feels both earned and unsettling, leaving the reader in a space of moral ambiguity — a fitting conclusion for a tale steeped in desire and vengeance. Wild does not provide easy answers, instead offering a provocative exploration of how far one might go in the name of love.

## Discussion launchpad
The tension within *Burn* lies not only in its romantic entanglements but also in its exploration of the boundaries between love and obsession. As we dissect the nuances of these relationships, we might find ourselves questioning the ethics of passion and revenge. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do the protagonists’ motivations reflect real-life complexities in relationships?  
- Where does the line blur between healthy desire and destructive obsession in the narrative?  
- Are the characters’ actions justified by their circumstances, or do they reveal deeper flaws?  
- How does the dual POV enhance or hinder the reader's connection to the characters?  
- What implications does the ending hold for our understanding of true love versus infatuation?
