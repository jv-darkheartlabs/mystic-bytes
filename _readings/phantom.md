## Hook & thesis
*Phantom* by H.D. Carlton is a visceral exploration of obsession that entwines the reader in a dance of desire and dread. **Verdict:** This is a hauntingly crafted narrative that delves deep into the shadows of the human psyche, revealing the dark allure of longing and possession.

## The contextual pivot
In the landscape of dark romance, H.D. Carlton stands as a contemporary architect of tension, weaving narratives that examine the tumultuous interplay between love and obsession. *Phantom*, situated within the stalker-obsession subgenre, not only adheres to its niche but also elevates it through literary finesse. Carlton's previous work, *Haunting Adeline*, set a precedent, and here she expands her exploration of psychological entanglement, crafting a world where the boundaries between affection and fixation are chillingly blurred. The invisible audience—those drawn to the darker shades of romance—will find familiarity here, yet Carlton’s prose invites a broader readership to contemplate the thrill of transgression.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Phantom* is both a labyrinth and a mirror; it reflects the characters' spiraling emotions while guiding the reader through a meticulously paced narrative. The dual perspectives employed create an unsettling intimacy, allowing readers to inhabit the minds of both the pursued and the pursuer. This choice enhances the thematic core, where vulnerability and power are perpetually in flux. Carlton's prose is lyrical yet visceral, employing sensory imagery that evokes a palpable atmosphere—smoke curling around roses, shadows dancing in dimly lit rooms—serving to heighten the tension between the characters.

Dialogue is sharp and often laden with subtext, revealing truths through silences and half-spoken words, which adds layers to the character dynamics. However, while the pacing is generally adept, certain sections linger too long on introspection, risking the momentum built earlier. That said, the ending is a masterstroke—convoluted yet earned—leaving the reader with a lingering sense of disquiet that resonates well beyond the final page. The thematic exploration of obsession and the thin line between love and madness is not just intriguing; it is an essential commentary on human desire's darker facets.

## Discussion launchpad
*Phantom* provokes a multitude of complex conversations: the nature of obsession, the ethics of desire, and the fine line between love and possession. What remains unspoken are the moral implications of the characters' choices and the societal reflections they embody. This tension invites readers to confront their own biases regarding love and obsession, asking us to consider where we draw the line.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Carlton balance romanticization with the darker aspects of obsession?  
- Are the characters relatable or irredeemably flawed, and what does that say about our understanding of desire?  
- In what ways does the dual POV enhance or detract from the narrative's impact?  
- What aspects of the ending felt inevitable, and how does that influence your view of the overall story?  
- How does the book challenge or reinforce societal perceptions of stalker narratives?
