## Hook & thesis
Raven Kennedy's *Gild* masterfully weaves a tale of captivity and desire, exploring the complexities of love entangled with power and possession. **Verdict:** This book is an intoxicating dance that reveals how the most exquisite cages can shackle the soul.

## The contextual pivot
*Gild* nestles itself in the dark romance subculture, yet it also appeals to a broader fantasy audience through its lush prose and intricate world-building. Drawing upon the Midas myth, Kennedy revitalizes themes of greed and transformation, intertwining them with a narrative that resonates deeply with readers who appreciate the nuances of fantasy romance. The book's appeal lies in its ability to bridge niche tropes—captivity, fae magic, and the interplay of light and dark—with universal emotions of longing and despair, making it accessible to those less familiar with darker narratives.

## Deep-dive critique
Kennedy employs a first-person POV that immerses the reader in the protagonist's psyche, enhancing the emotional intensity of the narrative. This choice effectively allows for a nuanced exploration of psychological captivity, where the gilded elements symbolize both the allure of love and the oppressive nature of control. The pacing, however, can occasionally falter, as the tension builds slowly, potentially testing the patience of readers accustomed to rapid-fire action. Yet, this deliberate pacing serves to deepen the thematic exploration of entrapment, mirroring the protagonist’s gradual awakening to her own desires. 

Moreover, Kennedy's prose is both lyrical and haunting, rich with imagery that evokes the opulence of the gilded cage while simultaneously hinting at its constraints. The dialogue crackles with tension, with exchanges that reveal hidden desires and unspoken fears, further enriching character dynamics. The ending, while poignant, leaves a thread of ambiguity that challenges the notion of liberation, asking whether true freedom can exist when intertwined with love. This complexity is what makes the journey through *Gild* both captivating and unsettling.

## Discussion launchpad
The thematic undercurrents of *Gild* raise essential questions about the nature of love and captivity. How does the concept of the 'gilded cage' resonate in our own lives, and what does it say about our desires? The interplay of fate and choice also invites scrutiny, making this a fertile ground for discussion.

**Questions for the room:**
- How does Kennedy's portrayal of captivity challenge our understanding of love and agency?
- What role does the fae mythology play in the development of the characters’ arcs?
- In what ways does the pacing affect your engagement with the story's themes?
- Does the ambiguous ending serve as a reflection of reality or is it a narrative misstep?
- How might the story change if told from a different character's perspective?
