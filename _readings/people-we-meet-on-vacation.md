## Hook & thesis
In **People We Meet on Vacation**, Emily Henry explores the complexities of friendship entangled with unspoken desire, stemming from years of shared vacations and unresolved tension. **Verdict:** This novel deftly balances the line between romantic yearning and emotional authenticity, rendering it a poignant exploration of love’s many faces.

## The contextual pivot
As a contemporary entry in the enemies-to-lovers trope, Henry crafts a narrative that not only embraces but subverts traditional romantic formulas. While readers familiar with her previous works may expect a lighter tone, this novel's layers of emotional depth echo the complexities found in the likes of Tessa Bailey and Helen Hoang, bridging niche romance to a broader audience. The book speaks to those who find solace in travel and the bittersweet nature of friendships that evolve into something deeper, appealing to both die-hard romance aficionados and casual readers seeking heartfelt connections.

## Deep-dive critique
Henry's prose shines with wit and relatability, allowing the reader to feel the weight of Poppy and Alex's shared experiences. The alternating timeline, juxtaposing present-day struggles with nostalgic recollections of past vacations, enhances the emotional stakes. However, the pacing sometimes falters; certain segments feel drawn out, weakening the urgency of their emotional revelations. The dialogue crackles with chemistry, yet at times, it glosses over the rawness of their unresolved feelings, leading to moments that verge on cliché instead of profound discovery. 

The thematic exploration of friendship as a precursor to romance is executed with finesse, but the character arcs occasionally lack the depth needed to fully justify their transitions. Poppy’s growth is compelling, but Alex's motivations often remain opaque, leaving readers craving a deeper understanding of his emotional barriers. The ending, while satisfying, could have benefitted from a more nuanced resolution, addressing lingering questions about their future, rather than tying everything up in a neat bow.

## Discussion launchpad
This narrative invites robust discussion on the balance between friendship and romance, particularly how the characters navigate their complex feelings. There are significant polarities regarding the pacing and character development. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do you interpret the evolution of Poppy and Alex's relationship throughout the novel?  
- In what ways do you think the setting contributes to the emotional stakes of their journey?  
- Did the ending resonate with you, or did it feel too predictable?  
- How does the book challenge or reinforce traditional enemies-to-lovers tropes?  
- What are the implications of unresolved feelings in friendships when it comes to romantic potential?
