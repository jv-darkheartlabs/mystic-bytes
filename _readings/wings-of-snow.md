## Hook & thesis
In *Wings of Snow*, Krista Street weaves a chilling tapestry of magic, desire, and danger, where the elements themselves become both a weapon and a balm. **Verdict:** This book encapsulates the tumultuous dance of passion and peril, ultimately revealing that love can either illuminate or freeze the heart.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled within the fantasy-romance genre, *Wings of Snow* emerges as a compelling entry that deftly combines elements of dark fantasy and royal intrigue, echoing the thematic complexities found in authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout. Street crafts a world steeped in winter's harsh beauty, inviting readers who often dwell in the realms of fated mates and enchanted landscapes. The narrative bridges niche tropes with a broader appeal, drawing in those who may not typically venture into the depths of fantasy; it captivates with its accessible prose while retaining the intricate layers of a richly built universe.

## Deep-dive critique
Street's craft shines particularly in her world-building, where the interplay of ice and magic serves as a metaphor for the characters' inner turmoil. The pacing, however, oscillates between moments of languid introspection and hurried action, which can occasionally undermine the tension. The choice of a dual POV allows readers to experience the intricacies of desire and fear through contrasting lenses; however, it sometimes leads to repetitive internal monologues that stall momentum. 

The thematic exploration of love as both a tether and a snare is beautifully executed, yet the character development can feel uneven. For instance, the protagonist's arc from vulnerability to empowerment is compelling, yet secondary characters occasionally lack depth, serving more as plot devices than fully fleshed-out individuals. Dialogue is sharp and engaging, yet moments of exposition can feel clunky, revealing a need for a more subtle hand in unfolding the story’s lore. The ending, while satisfying in its resolution of personal conflicts, leaves some larger narrative threads dangling, inviting speculation but risking reader frustration.

## Discussion launchpad
*Wings of Snow* is a tapestry of contrasts, inviting debate on the nature of love and the sacrifices it demands. While some may revel in the book's bold exploration of darker themes, others might find its romantic tropes predictable or its pacing disjointed.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do the themes of winter and magic enhance or detract from the characters' emotional journeys?  
- In what ways does the dual POV enrich or complicate the narrative?  
- Can the portrayal of royal intrigue be considered a fresh take, or does it fall into cliché?  
- How effectively does the book balance the elements of romance with the darker aspects of its fantasy setting?  
- What does the ending suggest about the nature of fate versus choice in love?
