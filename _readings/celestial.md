## Hook & thesis
*Celestial* by Olivia Wildenstein is a haunting exploration of desire and sacrifice, where the celestial and the grotesque intertwine in a dance of dark romance. **Verdict:** A masterclass in gothic tension, this novel seduces with its ethereal prose while exposing the raw, often dangerous desires that lie beneath the surface.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled within the gothic-horror-romance genre, *Celestial* shines as a contemporary reflection on angelic mythos, echoing the likes of Elizabeth Kostova's *The Historian* and the ethereal darkness of Anne Rice. Wildenstein’s narrative not only appeals to aficionados of supernatural love stories but also teases general readers with its accessible yet richly atmospheric prose. The author deftly bridges niche tropes—angelic beings and morbid allure—inviting readers to explore the liminal spaces where beauty and horror collide.

## Deep-dive critique
Wildenstein employs a dual narrative structure that oscillates between present-day and flashbacks, creating a tapestry of longing and regret. This choice deepens emotional resonance, allowing readers to grasp the complexity of the characters’ relationships and desires. The pacing is deliberate, drawing out the tension and leading to moments where desire ignites against the backdrop of foreboding. The prose is lush, almost poetic, imbued with imagery that evokes a visceral sense of place and feeling. However, at times, this richness can border on overwrought, potentially alienating readers who prefer a more straightforward narrative.

Characterization is where *Celestial* truly excels; the flawed, multi-dimensional figures grapple with their fates in ways that feel deeply human, even when wrapped in angelic wings. Dialogues are fraught with subtext, revealing the characters' inner struggles and unspoken desires. Yet, the world-building, while evocative, sometimes lacks clarity, leaving readers yearning for a more defined understanding of the celestial realm's rules and consequences.

The ending, a crescendo of emotion, is both satisfying and thought-provoking, posing questions about redemption and the cost of love. It leads us to reflect on whether the sacrifices made were worth the celestial price paid.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel’s exploration of the interplay between beauty and horror raises compelling questions about the nature of desire and sacrifice. What remains unsaid is the broader context of choice versus fate in relationships steeped in darkness.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Wildenstein’s portrayal of angelic beings challenge or conform to traditional gothic tropes?  
- In what ways does the dual narrative structure enhance or hinder the emotional impact of the story?  
- Can the moral complexities of the characters' choices create a rift in reader sympathy?  
- What do you think is the significance of the celestial imagery in relation to the darker themes of the novel?  
- How might the novel resonate differently for readers familiar with gothic romance versus those new to the genre?
