## Hook & thesis
In *Prince of Never*, Juno Heart immerses readers in a world where desire and peril intertwine, exploring the nuances of obsession within a fantastical realm. **Verdict:** This novel is a compelling exploration of power dynamics and forbidden love that leaves the reader breathless and questioning their own boundaries.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the fantasy-romance genre, *Prince of Never* deftly melds the enchantments of fae mythology with the visceral pull of dark romance. Heart’s work aligns with other modern authors such as Sarah J. Maas and Holly Black, who similarly navigate the treacherous waters of intrigue and passion. However, Heart's voice distinctly resonates with a darker undercurrent, appealing to readers who relish the complexities of obsession and royal power plays, while still inviting a broader audience into the lush, perilous landscapes she crafts.

## Deep-dive critique
Heart's structure, marked by a nonlinear timeline, effectively mirrors the tumultuous emotional states of her characters, intensifying the reader's engagement. The alternating points of view allow for a rich exploration of both protagonists' inner conflicts—each perspective deepening the tension and enhancing the stakes of their relationship. The prose is imbued with a lyrical quality that dances between dreamy allure and stark reality, capturing the essence of a world where beauty often masks danger. 

Character development stands out as a strength; the protagonists are flawed yet relatable, ensnared by their desires, which adds depth to their obsession. However, the pacing occasionally falters, particularly in the middle sections, where the buildup of tension risks feeling prolonged and repetitive. The dialogue, while poetic, sometimes veers into melodrama, which may alienate readers seeking authenticity over theatrics. 

The climax, rich with dark magic and royal intrigue, is both thrilling and thought-provoking, leaving the reader with an unsettled feeling that mirrors the characters' own struggles. Heart's ending is audacious and unorthodox, challenging the notion of traditional happily-ever-afters and provoking a deeper reflection on the costs of desire.

## Discussion launchpad
*Prince of Never* thrives on the tension between desire and danger, a duality that may split readers into fervent camps. How do we reconcile the allure of obsession with the inherent risks it poses? 

**Questions for the room:**  
- Do the characters’ actions justify their obsessive tendencies, or do they highlight a toxic narrative?  
- How does Heart’s portrayal of dark romance compare to more conventional love stories?  
- In what ways does the setting contribute to the themes of power and desire?  
- Can the ending be seen as empowering, or does it signify a surrender to fate?  
- How do the elements of fantasy enhance or detract from the romantic tension?
