## Hook & thesis
In *Wicked Beauty*, Katee Robert deftly intertwines desire with power in a retelling of the Trojan War, exploring how ambition can twist love into a weapon. **Verdict:** This novel thrives on the dangerous dance of polyamory and ambition, presenting a world where passion is not merely a backdrop but a raw and transformative force.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the fantasy-romance genre, *Wicked Beauty* emerges as a standout in Robert's *Dark Olympus* series, blending Greek mythology with contemporary themes of power dynamics and eroticism. Unlike typical mythological retellings that often romanticize or sanitize their subjects, Robert confronts the gritty realities of desire and ambition, appealing to both niche audiences captivated by myth and general readers drawn to complex interpersonal dynamics. At its core, the narrative speaks to a modern audience grappling with the implications of choice and desire in fluid relationships.

## Deep-dive critique
The novel is structured through a multi-POV lens, offering an immersive experience into the minds of its three central characters. This choice not only enhances the tension but also lays bare the conflicting desires that drive the plot. Robert's prose is both lyrical and visceral, expertly balancing moments of heat with the raw vulnerability of her characters. The pacing, particularly in the buildup to the climactic moments, mirrors the intensity of the stakes involved, making the emotional and physical connections feel urgent and consequential.

However, while the character development is robust, some moments teeter on the edge of melodrama, risking reader investment in the characters' fates. The world-building, rich with the lore of Olympus, sometimes falters in its depth, as certain elements are introduced but not fully explored, leaving readers wanting more context about the world’s rules. The ending, while dramatic, feels somewhat rushed, making it a point of contention regarding whether the resolution is as earned as it could have been.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of desire and ambition in *Wicked Beauty* raises questions about the nature of power within relationships, making it ripe for debate. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the portrayal of polyamory in the novel challenge or reinforce societal norms?  
- In what ways does the power imbalance between characters enhance or detract from the romantic tension?  
- Can the ending be considered satisfying if it feels rushed, and what does that say about narrative pacing in romance?  
- How does Robert's use of mythology influence our understanding of modern relationships depicted in the book?  
- What elements of desire are most compelling to you — emotional, physical, or a blend of both, and how are they represented here?
