## Hook & thesis
*Wicked Beauty* by Katee Robert sizzles with tension, thrusting readers into a retelling of the Trojan War that explores desire and power in a world steeped in myth and darkness. **Verdict:** This novel deftly marries the primal with the romantic, crafting a tale where the true conflict is not just love but the desperate, dangerous pursuits of agency against fate.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the burgeoning subgenre of dark fantasy romance, *Wicked Beauty* carves out a unique niche in the landscape of mythological retellings. Katee Robert, known for her intoxicating blends of eroticism and emotional depth, invites both devoted fans and newcomers to the *Dark Olympus* series into a sultry reinterpretation of classic characters. The novel's invisible audience comprises readers who crave complexity in their romantic entanglements, particularly those drawn to polyamory and moral ambiguity, pushing past typical genre boundaries to offer a tantalizing experience that resonates with broader audiences.

## Deep-dive critique
Robert's narrative excels in its structure, employing a close third-person perspective that allows readers to oscillate between the inner worlds of Helen, Achilles, and Patroclus, each character a vessel of desire and ambition. This shifting POV enhances the tension, as their motivations and vulnerabilities intertwine, creating a rich tapestry of emotional conflict. The pacing is meticulously crafted; moments of breathless passion collide with quieter, introspective scenes, ensuring that the heartbeat of the narrative remains palpable. 

The prose is sharp and evocative, dripping with sensuality while maintaining a gothic undertone that befits its mythological roots. Robert's dialogue is particularly noteworthy, crackling with wit and undercurrents of longing that reveal character depth without excessive exposition. However, the novel occasionally falters in its world-building; while the dark Olympus setting is compelling, some nuanced elements of its mythology could benefit from further exploration to ground the reader more firmly in this reimagined realm. 

The ending, while dramatic, raises questions about the emotional stakes — do the characters truly earn their resolutions, or do they simply align with the whims of fate? The tensions of love and desire become a double-edged sword, leaving readers both satisfied and longing for more.

## Discussion launchpad
While *Wicked Beauty* ignites passion and intrigue, it also opens the door to polarizing discussions around agency, consent, and the implications of polyamory in a mythological context. The interplay of power dynamics and desire can provoke debate on whether the characters' choices reflect true agency or are merely a response to their fateful destinies. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of polyamory enhance or detract from the central themes of power and agency?  
- How does the novel's setting influence your perception of the characters' choices?  
- In what ways does Robert challenge traditional romance tropes, and does she succeed?  
- What elements of the original myth do you think could have been explored further?  
- How does the tension between love and destiny resonate with modern readers?
