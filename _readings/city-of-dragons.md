## Hook & thesis
*City of Dragons* by Robin Hobb plunges us into a world where the bonds between humans and dragons are both a source of wonder and a breeding ground for deep-seated conflict. **Verdict:** Hobb masterfully entwines themes of longing and belonging within a fantastical tapestry, revealing that desire often comes at a perilous cost.

## The contextual pivot
In the sprawling landscape of fantasy romance, Robin Hobb stands as a titan, weaving complex narratives that explore the intertwined fates of her richly drawn characters. *City of Dragons*, the third installment in the Rain Wild Chronicles, continues to deliver Hobb's signature blend of intricate world-building and emotional depth. This work occupies a niche that balances the allure of dark romance with the broader appeal of high fantasy, making it accessible to both devoted fans of the genre and new readers drawn in by the promise of epic stakes and profound character exploration. Hobb’s ability to bridge the gap between niche tropes—such as forbidden love and existential despair—ensures that the narrative resonates widely, even with those unfamiliar with her previous works.

## Deep-dive critique
Hobb's structural prowess is evident in her layered narrative that navigates multiple perspectives, creating a rich tapestry of voices that enhance the emotional stakes. The pacing, though deliberate, serves to heighten tension; slow build-ups allow for an exploration of inner turmoil and desire, culminating in a climax that feels both inevitable and satisfying. The duality of the human-dragon relationship acts as a metaphor for the characters' struggles with their identities and desires, expertly mirrored in the dialogue that crackles with unspoken tension. Hobb’s prose is lush and evocative, skillfully painting the atmospheric backdrop of the Rain Wilds while never losing sight of her characters’ emotional landscapes.

However, some may argue that the pacing at times veers into languorous territory, potentially alienating readers eager for action. The dialogues, while richly textured, occasionally risk becoming overly introspective, which could detract from the narrative momentum. Still, the ending is earned—not merely a resolution of plot threads, but a poignant testament to the transformative power of connection and sacrifice, leaving readers with a bittersweet resonance that lingers long after the last page is turned.

## Discussion launchpad
Hobb's exploration of desire and belonging offers fertile ground for debate, especially regarding the balance between character introspection and plot progression. The juxtaposition of human frailty against the backdrop of mythical grandeur raises questions about the nature of sacrifice and the costs of desire.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Hobb's use of multiple POVs affect your emotional connection to the characters?  
- In what ways do you think the pacing served or hindered the themes of longing and desire?  
- Is the tension between human and dragon a metaphor that resonates beyond the fantasy genre?  
- How does the element of sacrifice redefine our understanding of love in this narrative?  
- What role does setting play in shaping the characters' internal conflicts?
