## Hook & thesis
In *City of Dragons*, Robin Hobb explores the unraveling threads of loyalty, identity, and the burdens of legacy against a richly crafted fantasy backdrop. **Verdict:** Hobb's masterful prose and profound character exploration elevate this installment into a haunting meditation on the costs of desire and the weight of history.

## The contextual pivot
Situated within the broader canvas of fantasy-romance, *City of Dragons* continues Hobb's acclaimed Rain Wild Chronicles, which delve into the complexities of dragon-human relationships and their societal implications. While fantasy often flirts with escapism, Hobb's work compels readers to confront the darker elements of human nature and connection, thus bridging niche tropes to a more general audience. This novel, like much of Hobb’s oeuvre, caters to readers who appreciate the intricacies of character development over mere world-building, positioning her as a cornerstone in modern fantasy literature alongside authors like N.K. Jemisin and Patrick Rothfuss, who similarly intertwine romance with profound thematic inquiries.

## Deep-dive critique
Hobb’s narrative structure in *City of Dragons* employs multiple perspectives, an effective choice that amplifies emotional stakes while simultaneously allowing readers to experience the dichotomy of desire and obligation through different lenses. The pacing is deliberate, creating a slow burn that mirrors the characters' internal struggles and the languid, often oppressive atmosphere of the Rain Wilds. This careful pacing serves to enhance the thematic richness of the narrative, allowing tension to simmer and ultimately explode in moments of heartbreak and revelation.

The prose is laced with Hobb's signature lyrical style, weaving evocative imagery with raw emotional depth. For instance, the vivid descriptions of the dragons and their environment not only serve as world-building but also reflect the characters' inner turmoil, drawing parallels between their struggles for acceptance and the dragons' fight for survival. This interconnection of character and setting underscores a key theme: the search for belonging in a world that often feels alien.

However, the dialogue, while often insightful, can occasionally tread into the territory of melodrama, risking moments of over-exposition that may detract from the otherwise immersive experience. Nonetheless, the intricate character arcs, particularly that of the protagonist, resonate with authenticity, making their journeys feel earned and deeply impactful.

## Discussion launchpad
Hobb's *City of Dragons* invites rich discussion about the nature of sacrifice and the complexity of relationships in a world fraught with danger and desire. The intertwining of personal and epic struggles creates a fertile ground for debate on the limits of loyalty and the morality of choices made in desperation.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Hobb’s portrayal of dragons as sentient beings affect your understanding of human relationships in the story?  
- In what ways does Hobb challenge traditional gender roles through her characters, and how does this resonate with contemporary readers?  
- Are the emotional stakes raised throughout the novel justified in the end, or do they feel contrived?  
- Discuss the balance between character-driven narrative and plot in Hobb's writing. Does the pacing serve to enhance or detract from the urgency of the story?
