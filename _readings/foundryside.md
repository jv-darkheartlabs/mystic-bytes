## Hook & thesis
**Foundryside** by Robert Jackson Bennett deftly intertwines the mechanics of magic with the complexities of human desire, ultimately revealing that the pursuit of freedom can lead to both creation and destruction. **Verdict:** This book is a thrilling exploration of agency and consequence, where the lines between villainy and virtue blur in the furnace of ambition.

## The contextual pivot
In the expansive realm of fantasy, *Foundryside* stands as a unique bridge between traditional adventure narratives and modern romantic undertones, echoing the work of authors like Brandon Sanderson and N.K. Jemisin. Bennett’s sophisticated melding of romantic elements into a world steeped in arcane technology positions this narrative not just as a story of magic, but as a profound commentary on societal constructs of power and love. The invisible audience here is likely a blend of dedicated fantasy enthusiasts and those seeking deeper emotional resonance, as Bennett manages to weave niche tropes into a tapestry that invites general readers to reconsider the implications of their own desires and moral choices.

## Deep-dive critique
Bennett’s structure in *Foundryside* employs a fast-paced, multi-POV approach that deftly alternates between characters, enhancing the tension and allowing the reader to experience the diverse motivations driving the narrative. The pacing is relentless, with each chapter ending on a note that compels the reader forward, mirroring the characters’ own relentless pursuits. The world-building is particularly commendable; Bennett crafts a vibrant, industrial city where magic is treated as a tangible resource, reflecting real-world issues of exploitation and technology’s moral implications. 

However, while the dialogue sparkles with wit and emotional undercurrents, some character arcs feel slightly rushed, particularly in the latter stages of the book. The resolution, though satisfying, leaves certain thematic threads dangling, inviting the reader to contemplate the cost of ambition and the price of true freedom. In this way, the ending is both earned and thought-provoking, stimulating further discussion on the nature of power and personal agency.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay between technology and magic raises questions about morality and the consequences of innovation. Bennett’s exploration of desire, both personal and societal, invites a rich debate on what lengths one should go to for freedom. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Bennett’s portrayal of magic as a form of technology influence your understanding of character motivations?  
- In what ways do the characters’ desires reflect or subvert traditional fantasy tropes?  
- Can you identify specific moments where the pacing undermines character development?  
- What thematic elements do you believe were left unresolved, and how do they affect your overall view of the narrative?  
- How does the blending of romantic and fantastical elements enhance or detract from the story's core message?
