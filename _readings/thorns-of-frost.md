## Hook & thesis
In *Thorns of Frost*, Krista Street masterfully intertwines desire and danger, crafting a tale where love is as treacherous as the icy thorns that define its world. **Verdict:** This novel chills the heart while igniting a fierce longing, proving that in a landscape of frost, passion can burn incandescently.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned firmly within the fantasy-romance genre, *Thorns of Frost* serves as the second installment in the *Of Snow & Ice* series, following in the frosted footsteps of predecessors like Sarah J. Maas’s *A Court of Thorns and Roses*. Street's work bridges niche tropes—such as fey politics and winter curses—with a broader audience's yearning for poignant emotional stakes, making it a compelling read for both lovers of gothic romance and fantasy enthusiasts alike.

## Deep-dive critique
Street’s narrative is a study in contrasts, with its pacing reflecting the chill of its setting while simultaneously echoing the heat of its central romance. The dual perspective allows for an intimate exploration of both protagonists' desires and fears, effectively enhancing the tension that drives the story. The dialogue, laced with wit and yearning, acts as a vehicle for character development, revealing vulnerabilities beneath bravado. Yet, where the prose excels in painting a rich, immersive world, it occasionally falters in moments of exposition that feel heavy-handed, undermining the otherwise fluid pacing. The thematic exploration of sacrifice and the cost of love is deftly woven through the characters’ arcs, culminating in an ending that feels both satisfying and inevitable, yet leaves a trace of ambiguity—perhaps a reflection of the enduring winter that blankets their world.

## Discussion launchpad
The juxtaposition of beauty and danger permeates the narrative, prompting readers to consider the nature of love amidst peril. How does Street challenge traditional romance tropes within the fantasy genre? What is left unsaid about the emotional costs of desire and the societal expectations that bind the characters?

- In what ways do the themes resonate with contemporary issues in relationships?
