## Hook & thesis  
In *Stinger*, Mia Sheridan deftly navigates the treacherous waters of forbidden love, painting a portrait of desire that is both intoxicating and claustrophobic. **Verdict:** This novel is a masterclass in the perilous dance between longing and consequence, compelling readers to confront the seductive nature of transgression.

## The contextual pivot  
Situated squarely within the forbidden-love genre, *Stinger* joins the ranks of notable works that explore the tension of societal boundaries and personal desire, reminiscent of authors like Colleen Hoover and Anna Todd. Sheridan's signature style is steeped in emotional depth and moral ambiguity, establishing a narrative that appeals not only to romance aficionados but also to a broader audience seeking complexity. The Las Vegas backdrop — a city of illusion and vice — becomes a character in its own right, amplifying the stakes for the characters ensnared in their passions.

## Deep-dive critique  
*Stinger* excels in its structure, employing a dual POV that allows readers to intimately experience the inner turmoil of both protagonists, which enhances the tension inherent in their forbidden connection. The pacing deftly oscillates between simmering moments of anticipation and explosive revelations, crafting a rhythm that mirrors the characters' escalating desires. Sheridan's prose is lush yet precise, evoking the neon vibrancy of Las Vegas while grounding the narrative in raw emotional authenticity. 

Character development shines as Sheridan delves into the protagonists' backstories, revealing the shadows that drive their choices and the societal constraints they grapple with. Dialogue is sharp and revealing; exchanges pulse with subtext, each word heavy with unsaid implications. However, some readers may find the resolution slightly rushed, with certain character arcs feeling inadequately resolved against the backdrop of their intense emotional journeys. The ending, while provocative, invites scrutiny regarding whether it truly honors the complexities established throughout the narrative.

## Discussion launchpad  
*Stinger* is rife with polarizing elements, including the moral implications of the protagonists' choices and the societal critique woven through their love story. The novel challenges readers to ponder the cost of desire and the boundaries one is willing to cross.

- In what ways does Sheridan's approach to forbidden love differ from other authors in the genre?  
- How do the dual POVs shape your understanding of desire and consequence in the narrative?
