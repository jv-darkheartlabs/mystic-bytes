## Hook & thesis
*Neon Gods* by Katee Robert reimagines the Hades and Persephone myth through a lens of contemporary urban allure and visceral desire. **Verdict:** This novel is a tantalizing exploration of power dynamics and the intoxicating pull of forbidden love, wrapped in a neon-lit narrative that effectively ignites the senses.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the realm of fantasy romance, *Neon Gods* diverges from traditional myth retellings by enveloping its characters in a gritty, modern setting that pulses with life and danger. Robert's work often blends dark romance with mythological themes, and here she deepens that exploration, drawing parallels with authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout, who craft worlds where desire and danger coalesce seamlessly. The invisible audience for this novel is both the genre's devoted followers and a general readership seeking a fresh, electrifying entry point into mythic narratives, effortlessly bridging niche tropes with broader appeal.

## Deep-dive critique
Robert's narrative structure thrives on a dual perspective that invites readers into the minds of both Hades and Persephone, enhancing the emotional stakes and complicating their relationship. The pacing is deliberate yet thrilling, allowing readers to savor the slow burn of attraction before the inevitable conflagration. This method creates tension that radiates off the pages, making the moments of connection feel both electrifying and dangerous. 

The prose itself is sharp and vivid, with dialogue that crackles, revealing character depth through wit and vulnerability. Robert excels in world-building, crafting a vibrant cityscape that serves as a character in its own right—one that seduces and ensnares, mirroring the characters’ tumultuous relationship. Yet, while the ending provides a satisfying resolution to the central conflict, it risks undermining the darker themes of power and autonomy that permeate the narrative; the conclusion, while emotionally charged, leaves some thematic threads dangling, inviting scrutiny.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel's allure lies not only in its romantic entanglements but in its exploration of power and autonomy—elements that may polarize readers based on their interpretations of consent and agency within the context of desire. Such complexities prompt a deeper examination of the mythic underpinnings and how they resonate with contemporary issues of empowerment and control.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How effectively does the dual perspective enhance or complicate the themes of power and consent?  
- In what ways does the urban setting influence the characterization of Hades and Persephone?  
- Does the ending of *Neon Gods* feel earned in the context of the overarching themes?  
- How does the tension between mythology and modernity play out in the narrative?  
- What implications does the portrayal of desire in this retelling have for our understanding of the original myth?
