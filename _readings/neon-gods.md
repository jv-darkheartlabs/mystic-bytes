## Hook & thesis
In **Neon Gods**, Katee Robert deftly reimagines the Hades and Persephone myth, transforming it into a pulsating exploration of desire, power dynamics, and the weight of choice amidst a backdrop of neon-lit intrigue. **Verdict:** Robert's bold retelling transcends genre conventions, offering a visceral experience that lingers long after the final page.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled within the burgeoning subgenre of fantasy romance, **Neon Gods** stands as a daring retake of one of mythology's most enduring tales. Robert’s work resonates within a continuum of modern adaptations that explore the dualities of love and power, recalling the likes of A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Maas, yet it carves its own niche with a fresh urban aesthetic and a more pronounced examination of obsession and consent. The target audience here isn’t just die-hard fans of myth retellings but also readers craving a contemporary lens on classic narratives — a bridge that invites both the casual romantic and the seasoned fantasy reader.

## Deep-dive critique
Robert's narrative structure is compelling, utilizing a dual POV that allows for intimate insights into both Hades and Persephone. This choice not only deepens the reader's understanding of their complex motivations but also amplifies the tension inherent in their power imbalance. The pacing is deliberate, shifting seamlessly between moments of breathless passion and chilling realizations, which serves to heighten the stakes at every turn. However, there are moments where the dialogue feels somewhat stilted, particularly during key revelations that could benefit from more nuance. 

The world-building, crafted with vibrant detail, encapsulates a dark, modern underworld that juxtaposes the mythic with the mundane; the neon aesthetics serve not just as decoration but as a reflection of the characters' inner turmoil. Yet, the thematic success of the novel lies in how it grapples with consent and obsession, skillfully navigating the murky waters of attraction and manipulation. By the conclusion, the transformation of both leads feels earned, though it may leave readers deliberating the ethical implications of their choices. 

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of power and desire in **Neon Gods** will likely ignite passionate debate among readers. The novel raises questions about the ethics of relationships forged in such imbalanced dynamics, as well as the nature of love in contexts that blur lines between agency and obsession.  

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do we reconcile the romanticized portrayal of power imbalances in this narrative?  
- In what ways does the modern setting influence your perception of the mythic elements?  
- Can we separate the themes of obsession and desire, or are they inextricably linked in this context?  
- What are the implications of the character transformations — do they signify growth or complicity?  
- How effective is the dual POV in deepening the emotional stakes of the story?
