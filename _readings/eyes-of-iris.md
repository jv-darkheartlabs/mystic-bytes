## Hook & thesis
*Eyes of Iris* by Joshua A.H. Harris is a disorienting exploration of identity and perception, where reality is a malleable construct shaped by desire and dread. **Verdict:** This novel masterfully distorts reality, inviting readers to confront the unsettling dynamics of selfhood and obsession.

## The contextual pivot
In the dark-thriller landscape, *Eyes of Iris* stands out as a psychological labyrinth, echoing the complexities of works by authors such as Shirley Jackson and Thomas Ligotti. Harris weaves surreal elements into his narrative, creating a tapestry that challenges both genre conventions and reader expectations. His previous works suggest a preoccupation with the uncanny and the fractured self, yet here he deepens that exploration, bridging niche tropes with a broader audience intrigued by the psychological undercurrents of suspense. The invisible reader is not merely seeking thrills but is drawn into a cerebral confrontation with fear and desire.

## Deep-dive critique
Harris's structuring of *Eyes of Iris* reflects the instability of its protagonist’s psyche, employing a non-linear timeline and shifting POV that fracture the narrative much like the identity of its central character. This choice amplifies the disorientation, pulling readers into a swirling vortex of perception where truth is as elusive as the shadows that haunt the protagonist. The pacing, at first frenetic, gradually shifts to a more contemplative rhythm, mirroring the protagonist's descent into introspection and paranoia. The prose is laden with vivid imagery and haunting metaphors, often juxtaposing beauty with grotesquery — a stylistic choice that enhances the thematic tension of desire entwined with dread.

Dialogue is sparse but impactful, often dripping with subtext and dual meanings that echo the protagonist’s fractured mind. While the thematic ambition is commendable, the mechanical execution falters at times; certain plot threads feel underdeveloped, leaving the reader craving deeper exploration of crucial elements, particularly the motivations behind key characters. The ending, though enigmatic, risks alienating readers who seek resolution, as it opens more questions than it answers, encapsulating the novel’s exploration of the unfathomable depths of the human psyche.

## Discussion launchpad
*Eyes of Iris* is a provocative work that invites debate over its thematic choices and narrative structure. The polarizing elements lie in its approach to identity and the surreal; some may find the disjointed narrative a reflection of modern existence, while others may see it as a misstep. What remains unsaid in this exploration of selfhood and perception could fuel a riveting discussion about our own identities shaped by external forces.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do you interpret the use of surrealism in depicting psychological trauma?  
- Is the non-linear structure an effective tool for exploring identity, or does it hinder reader engagement?  
- What do you think Harris is saying about the nature of desire and its impact on identity?  
- How does the sparse dialogue affect your connection to the characters?  
- Does the unresolved ending enrich or diminish the overall experience of the novel?
