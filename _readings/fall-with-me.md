## Hook & thesis
In Becka Mack's *Fall with Me*, the true engine driving the narrative is the tumultuous dance of vulnerability and trust that emerges from the clash between two fierce rivals. **Verdict:** This book seduces with its intricate exploration of emotional barriers, making it a standout in the enemies-to-lovers genre.

## The contextual pivot
*Fall with Me* nestles comfortably within the broader landscape of contemporary dark romance, particularly the enemies-to-lovers trope that has gained traction in recent years. Mack's work resonates with readers who find solace in the tension of conflicting emotions, which is a hallmark of her writing. Compared to authors like Penelope Douglas and Tessa Bailey, Mack skillfully bridges niche romantic tropes while maintaining accessibility for a wider audience, ensuring that both die-hard fans and new readers feel the heat of her characters' encounters. The novel’s intimate exploration of themes such as past trauma also places it among the more emotionally complex offerings of sports romances, inviting a deeper connection with its audience.

## Deep-dive critique
Mack employs a dual first-person POV that allows for an intimate exploration of both protagonists, enhancing the emotional stakes of their rivalry. The pacing is sharp, oscillating between moments of simmering tension and explosive confrontations that draw readers into the intensity of their relationship. The use of dialogue is particularly noteworthy; it crackles with an underlying current of desire and unspoken fears, making every exchange feel loaded and significant. Mack’s prose excels in creating a vivid world around the sport of hockey, where the icy veneer of competition contrasts beautifully with the warmth of budding romance. However, while the character development is largely successful, some secondary characters felt underdeveloped, creating a slight imbalance in the narrative's emotional landscape. The resolution, while satisfying, treads familiar territory, risking predictability amidst the vibrant backdrop of the protagonists' journey.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of rivalry and romance raises intriguing questions about the nature of love in adversarial contexts, inviting varied interpretations of the characters' motivations and growth. 
**Questions for the room:**  
- What impact do the characters' past traumas have on their ability to connect — do we see growth or merely repetition of cycles?  
- How does Mack balance tension and resolution, and does it ultimately serve the story?  
- Can the predictability of the ending detract from the overall experience, or does it lend a comforting familiarity to the genre?  
- What role does the sport of hockey play in shaping the characters and their relationship dynamics?  
- In what ways does the book subvert or conform to traditional enemies-to-lovers tropes?
