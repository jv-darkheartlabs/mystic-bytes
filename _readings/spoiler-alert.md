## Hook & thesis
In Olivia Dade's **Spoiler Alert**, the interplay between public persona and private desire forms the novel's core, illuminating the complexities of identity and vulnerability. **Verdict:** This book masterfully navigates the choppy waters of societal expectation and personal longing, laying bare the heart's true desires beneath a cozy rom-com exterior.

## The contextual pivot
As an entry in the enemies-to-lovers sub-genre, **Spoiler Alert** intertwines the thematic elements of celebrity culture and online toxicity with a nuanced exploration of body image, particularly through its portrayal of plus-size representation. Dade, known for her sharp wit and keen social insights, offers a refreshing take on familiar tropes, positioning her work amidst contemporaries like Jasmine Guillory and Talia Hibbert, while also appealing to a broader audience drawn to romance that digs deeper than mere escapism. The novel serves not only fans of romantic comedy but also those who seek narratives that challenge societal norms and celebrate diverse identities.

## Deep-dive critique
Dade's structure, oscillating between witty banter and deep introspection, deftly maintains a rhythm that reflects the push and pull of the relationship at its heart. The dual perspective enriches the reader's understanding of both protagonists, allowing their vulnerabilities and strengths to surface organically. Characterization shines particularly in the way Dade crafts her leads—both shaped by their public personas yet wholly relatable in their flaws and fears. For instance, the protagonist's journey through online harassment adds a layer of urgency and relevance to the romantic stakes, pushing the narrative beyond a simple love story.

Dialogue sparkles with authenticity, peppered with humor that reveals character depth rather than simply serving to entertain. However, while the humor often lands, some scenes venture into predictability, where stakes appear less compelling amid the often light-hearted tone. The ending, though satisfying, could be seen as a somewhat safe resolution, lacking the boldness that the earlier narrative promised.

## Discussion launchpad
Dade's work invites readers to confront the friction between public image and private self, raising questions about the authenticity of relationships in a social media age. This tension, combined with the novel's exploration of body positivity and online harassment, sets the stage for rich discussion. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Dade's portrayal of online harassment enhance or detract from the romantic elements of the story?  
- In what ways does the representation of plus-size characters challenge traditional romantic tropes?  
- Is the resolution of the protagonists' conflict satisfying, or does it lean too heavily into genre conventions?  
- How does humor function in the narrative, and does it overshadow deeper themes?  
- What might be the implications of setting a romance against the backdrop of celebrity culture in today's social climate?
