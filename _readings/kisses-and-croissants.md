## Hook & thesis
In *Kisses and Croissants*, Anne-Sophie Jouhanneau deftly explores the volatile tension between ambition and romance within the enchanting yet treacherous landscape of Parisian life. **Verdict:** This enemies-to-lovers tale serves as a sharp reminder that the pursuit of one’s dreams can be as intoxicating as it is perilous, with desires igniting both passion and conflict.

## The contextual pivot
This novel sits comfortably within the contemporary romance genre, particularly the enemies-to-lovers trope, which has gained significant traction in recent years. Jouhanneau’s work draws parallels to authors like Emily Henry and Talia Hibbert, who similarly blend humor and heartfelt emotion within romantic frameworks. The invisible audience presumably includes a younger demographic with a penchant for escapism, yet Jouhanneau's nuanced characterizations and vivid Parisian settings invite more general readers to engage with the text. The novel bridges niche themes of culinary artistry and rivalry in a way that feels accessible, while still appealing to those familiar with the complexities of young love.

## Deep-dive critique
At its core, *Kisses and Croissants* thrives on the juxtaposition of ambition and desire, skillfully using structure to enhance its thematic exploration. The alternating perspectives between the protagonists provide readers insight into their internal conflicts, allowing for a richer understanding of their motivations and desires. This duality is well-paced, injecting tension into the narrative that mirrors the heat of their growing attraction. Jouhanneau’s prose is lively and evocative, effectively painting the romantic backdrop of Paris as both a dreamscape and a battleground where love and ambition collide. 

However, the dialogue occasionally falters, leaning into clichés that can detract from character depth. While the protagonists are compelling, some supporting characters feel underdeveloped, serving merely as plot devices rather than contributing to the thematic richness. Moreover, the resolution, while satisfactory, lacks the emotional punch that might have made it feel fully earned. The stakes could have been heightened further, as Jouhanneau sets up a tantalizing premise but sometimes shies away from fully exploring the darker implications of ambition at odds with personal relationships.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel's engaging exploration of desire versus ambition invites a variety of interpretations, particularly in how love is portrayed against the backdrop of personal goals. This contradiction can either resonate deeply or feel superficial, depending on the reader's perspective. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the Parisian setting influence the characters’ journey and their relationship?  
- Are the conflicts presented in the story more about personal ambition or relational dynamics?  
- To what extent do you feel the supporting characters enrich or dilute the main narrative?  
- Does Jouhanneau succeed in portraying genuine tension between love and ambition, or does it come off as romanticized?  
- How might a more complex ending alter the impact of the story?
