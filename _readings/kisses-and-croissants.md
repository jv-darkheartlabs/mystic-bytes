## Hook & thesis
In *Kisses and Croissants*, Anne-Sophie Jouhanneau crafts a delectable enemies-to-lovers narrative that artfully intertwines ambition and rivalry against the enchanting backdrop of Parisian patisserie. **Verdict:** This book proves that the sweetest romances often rise from the flour-dusted ashes of conflict and competition.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the vibrant landscape of contemporary romance, *Kisses and Croissants* embraces the enemies-to-lovers trope with a fresh perspective that feels at home alongside works like *The Hating Game* by Sally Thorne and *Beach Read* by Emily Henry. Jouhanneau’s portrayal of rival bakers highlights both the culinary artistry and emotional intricacies present in the genre. This book appeals to a broad audience—those who thrive on the tension of romantic rivalry while skillfully bridging niche elements of culinary obsession with universal themes of love and ambition, rendering it accessible to readers beyond the genre’s typical confines.

## Deep-dive critique
Jouhanneau’s structure employs a dual point-of-view, allowing readers to delve into the minds of both protagonists, which not only amplifies the romantic tension but also deepens the reader's investment in their individual journeys. The pacing is expertly managed; the tension simmers gently before reaching a satisfying crescendo, with moments of levity punctuating the rising stakes. The dialogue crackles with a mix of playful banter and raw honesty, effectively capturing the push-and-pull dynamic inherent in their relationship. 

Thematically, *Kisses and Croissants* excels in exploring the thin line between love and rivalry, underscored by the characters’ personal ambitions that often clash with their burgeoning feelings. The setting—Paris, with its culinary delights—serves not just as a backdrop, but as a character in its own right, enriching the narrative with layers of sensuality that evoke desire without overt depiction. However, the book does have its mechanical flaws; certain plot points feel predictable, and while the ending is heartwarming, it may lack the complexity some readers crave. Nevertheless, the emotional arcs of the characters feel earned, as they navigate their past conflicts and emerge transformed.

## Discussion launchpad
As we unravel the layers of *Kisses and Croissants*, several polarities emerge that could ignite fervent debate among us. The delicate balance between ambition and romance raises questions about prioritizing personal goals versus relationships. Moreover, the portrayal of rivalry—does it enhance or detract from the romance?

- In what ways do you think ambition should influence romantic relationships, based on the characters' journeys?  
- What does this novel suggest about the complexities of pursuing dreams in the face of love?
