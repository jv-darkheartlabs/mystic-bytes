## Hook & thesis
Abby Jimenez’s *Say You'll Remember Me* intricately weaves a tale of forbidden love, where time itself becomes the cruelest antagonist. **Verdict:** This novel thrives on the tension of fleeting moments and the weight of memory, leaving readers entranced yet yearning for resolution.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled firmly within the forbidden love genre, Jimenez’s work engages with contemporary themes of connection and impermanence, reminiscent of the emotional landscapes crafted by authors like Colleen Hoover and Taylor Jenkins Reid. This narrative is not just a road trip; it’s a pilgrimage through emotional terrain that explores the gap between desire and circumstance, appealing to both niche readers and those seeking broader romantic themes. Jimenez’s ability to bridge the intimate and the universal speaks to a growing audience hungry for complexity within romance.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Say You'll Remember Me* serves as a compelling canvas for its themes. The alternating POVs create an intimate yet fragmented experience, allowing readers to oscillate between the characters’ internal conflicts. This duality enhances the tension, peppering the narrative with layers of longing and regret that echo the ticking clock motif. The pacing, particularly during pivotal moments of connection, is deliberate, building an atmosphere thick with unfulfilled desires that feels both electric and achingly real.

Jimenez excels in crafting dialogue that is both poignant and humorous, providing levity in moments of deep emotional struggle. The characters are fleshed out with vulnerabilities that resonate; they are not mere archetypes but reflections of fear and hope. However, certain secondary characters feel underdeveloped, a missed opportunity to enrich the narrative landscape. The ending, while satisfying, could leave some readers wanting a deeper exploration of the consequences of their choices, as it skims over potential fallout for a more romantic resolution, which may feel slightly unearned for an audience attuned to darker themes. 

## Discussion launchpad
Despite its engaging narrative, the novel raises questions about the nature of love that feels transient — is it worth pursuing if it cannot endure? The juxtaposition of light-hearted moments against darker undertones creates a push and pull that may leave readers divided on the story’s emotional honesty.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do the characters embody the struggle between desire and the fear of loss?  
- Do you believe the ending is a true reflection of their journey, or does it undermine the darker themes introduced?  
- How does the use of dual POV affect your connection to the characters?  
- In what ways does the road trip motif serve the larger themes of the book?  
- Can love that is bound by time still be considered authentic, or does it diminish its value?
