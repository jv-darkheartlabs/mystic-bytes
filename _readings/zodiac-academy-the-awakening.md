## Hook & thesis
"Zodiac Academy: The Awakening" is a fierce exploration of power dynamics and identity within a world shaped by celestial hierarchies and brutal rivalries. **Verdict:** This book expertly fuels desire through conflict, crafting a seductive narrative that ensnares readers in a web of dark ambition and relentless rivalry.

## The contextual pivot
Nestled within the bully-romance subgenre, this novel occupies a unique niche that merges the allure of magical academies with the tantalizing complexity of fae mythology. Peckham and Valenti's work stands alongside the likes of Jennifer Armentrout and S.J. Maas, who similarly weave intricate worlds where desire collides with danger. The authors deftly bridge the gap between niche and general readership by enveloping familiar tropes—such as enemies-to-lovers dynamics and power struggles—within a richly developed setting that invites both seasoned readers and newcomers to indulge in its dark charms.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of "Zodiac Academy" expertly mirrors its thematic preoccupations, with a pacing that oscillates between frenetic confrontations and intimate character moments. The dual POV allows readers to navigate the complexities of the sisters' experiences, effectively contrasting their individual struggles against the backdrop of the academy's unforgiving hierarchy. Characterization is a highlight; each bully is imbued with tantalizing layers that shift between antagonism and attraction, making their interactions electric. The dialogue crackles with tension, showcasing wit and veiled threats that amplify the burgeoning desire between characters.

However, the book is not without its flaws. At times, the plot's reliance on familiar tropes can feel predictable, risking reader fatigue. Certain character arcs may lack the depth needed to render their motivations fully believable, particularly as the stakes escalate. Yet, the authors manage to craft a satisfying conclusion that hints at further complexities, leaving the door ajar for future exploration without feeling completely unearned.

## Discussion launchpad
While the book excels in creating an immersive experience, it also raises questions about the dynamics of power and consent within the narrative. The fine line between bullying and romance can provoke differing interpretations among readers, challenging the comfort zones of the audience.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do the authors balance the line between romantic tension and toxic dynamics?  
- In what ways do the character relationships reflect real-world power struggles?  
- Are there narrative moments that feel gratuitous versus necessary for character development?  
- How does the world-building enhance or detract from the romantic tension?  
- What tropes in the bully-romance genre resonate most with you, and why?
