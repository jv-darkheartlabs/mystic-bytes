## Hook & thesis
In *Zodiac Academy: The Awakening*, Caroline Peckham and Susanne Valenti weave a tale of power and desire set against a backdrop of supernatural rivalry. **Verdict:** This book thrives on the electrifying tension of its bully-romance trope, pulsating with a darker undercurrent that challenges conventional notions of love and intimacy.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the burgeoning realm of bully romance, this novel aligns itself with thematic forebears like *Cruel Intentions* and *The Cruel Prince*, yet distinguishes itself with a richly developed magical academy setting reminiscent of *Harry Potter* and *A Court of Thorns and Roses*. The authors deftly merge niche tropes of dark fantasy with a broader audience appeal, creating a narrative that tantalizes avid readers of both young adult and adult fiction. By infusing the story with elements of fae lore and high-stakes conflict, the book bridges the gap between a specialized fandom and newcomers, making it a compelling entry point into the genre.

## Deep-dive critique
Peckham and Valenti's novel excels in its pacing, harnessing a structure that intersperses rapid action with moments of introspection, allowing readers to grapple with the duality of attraction and animosity. The alternating points of view deepen the reader's connection to both protagonists, each embodying conflicting desires that reflect the broader theme of power dynamics in relationships. The prose is rich and evocative, often juxtaposing the beauty of the fae world against the brutal realities of the characters' interactions.

However, the dialogue occasionally falters, veering into cliché territory that detracts from the otherwise immersive experience. Characters oscillate between being compellingly complex and bordering on caricature, notably in moments of heightened emotion where motivations can feel overly simplified. The starkness of the ending raises questions about character growth; while the stakes are undeniably high, one wonders if the resolution is sufficiently earned given the tumultuous journey.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of power and vulnerability in *Zodiac Academy: The Awakening* invites both admiration and critique, particularly regarding the portrayal of bullying as a precursor to intimacy. The moral implications of these dynamics may provoke fierce debate among readers.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the portrayal of bullying in the story shape our understanding of the characters’ relationships?  
- In what ways does the magical setting enhance or detract from the central themes of power and desire?  
- Are the characters’ actions justified in the context of their world, or do they perpetuate harmful tropes?  
- How effectively does the dual POV contribute to the tension and resolution of the story?  
- What unresolved threads leave you questioning the authors’ intentions and the characters’ futures?
