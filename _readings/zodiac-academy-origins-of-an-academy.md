## Hook & thesis  
In "Zodiac Academy: Origins of an Academy," Caroline Peckham and Susanne Valenti deftly weave a tale of ambition shadowed by cruelty, exploring the tumultuous intersection of power dynamics and desire in a fantastical setting. **Verdict:** This novella vibrates with tension, drawing readers into a world where the thrill of ambition is as intoxicating as the sting of betrayal.

## The contextual pivot  
Nestled within the burgeoning realm of bully-romance, this prequel serves as both a gateway and an extension of the Zodiac Academy series, which has captivated a niche yet robust audience. It embraces the archetypal themes of dark academia and supernatural rivalry, reminiscent of works by authors like L.J. Smith and Sarah J. Maas, yet it carves its own identity through a more intimate focus on the interpersonal conflicts that fuel its plot. The invisible audience here seems split between those who relish the sharp edges of toxic relationships and readers who may shy away from such themes, yet the authors skillfully navigate these waters, making the narrative accessible to a broader readership.

## Deep-dive critique  
This novella's structure employs a tight, linear timeline, creating a sense of immediacy that heightens the narrative’s stakes. The first-person perspectives allow for an intimate glimpse into the characters' minds, yet at times this closeness restricts the exploration of broader world-building, leaving elements of the academy's intricate lore only half-formed. The prose is sharp and evocative; Peckham and Valenti excel at creating visceral imagery that underscores the emotional landscape — feelings of desire are often layered with an undercurrent of danger, effectively mirroring the characters' tumultuous relationships. 

Dialogue, though occasionally veering into cliché, captures the fierce banter typical of bully-romance, which can either charm or alienate readers depending on their tastes. The thematic exploration of power, loyalty, and betrayal resonates throughout, yet the conclusion, while impactful, feels rushed. The emotional arcs of the characters culminate in a climax that hints at future conflicts, leaving the reader craving resolution but not entirely fulfilled. This ambivalence serves the genre’s appetite for tension, but may frustrate those seeking a more rounded narrative closure.

## Discussion launchpad  
The novella's exploration of power imbalance and the allure of toxicity invites a spectrum of reactions. Readers may find themselves divided over the portrayal of relationships rooted in bullying and manipulation, raising critical questions about morality and consent in romance. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the novella romanticize toxic relationships, and if so, does it do so intentionally?  
- How does the setting of an academy amplify the themes of rivalry and ambition?  
- In what ways do the characters' motivations reflect broader societal issues?  
- What impact does the first-person narrative have on our perception of the characters?  
- Is the novella's ending satisfying in the context of its genre, or does it leave too many threads unresolved?
