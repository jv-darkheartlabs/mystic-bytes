## Hook & thesis
In *Business or Pleasure*, Rachel Lynn Solomon deftly intertwines the realms of ambition and desire, scrutinizing the treacherous dance between professional aspirations and personal longing. **Verdict:** This novel is a scintillating exploration of the complexities of intimacy and ambition, ultimately positioning desire as a powerful force that can both illuminate and obscure our true intentions.

## The contextual pivot
Set against a vibrant backdrop of contemporary fantasy romance, *Business or Pleasure* navigates well-trodden tropes of workplace romance yet infuses them with a unique blend of wit and tension. Solomon's previous works often blend humor with heart, but here she escalates emotional stakes, making the invisible audience—those who relish not just romance but an introspective journey—feel seen. This book stands confidently within the genre, drawing readers familiar with the likes of Helen Hoang and Talia Hibbert, yet it carves its own niche with a haunting exploration of what it means to straddle the line between business obligations and fervent connection.

## Deep-dive critique
Solomon's craft shines in her mastery of dialogue, which crackles with an electric tension that mirrors the protagonists’ precarious balancing act. The structure is cleverly interwoven, alternating perspectives that allow readers to experience the inner turmoil of both characters as they grapple with their desires. This duality serves not only to build suspense but also to deepen the emotional resonance of their conflicts. However, the pacing occasionally falters, particularly in moments where the narrative lingers too long on tangential thoughts, risking a loss of momentum. The world-building is vibrant, establishing a fantastical element that enriches the setting without overshadowing the character-driven narrative.

Thematically, *Business or Pleasure* excels in portraying the nuances of consent and agency, cleverly positioning the characters’ professional negotiations as a metaphor for their romantic entanglements. Yet, the climax, while satisfying, feels slightly rushed, leaving some threads tantalizingly unresolved, challenging the reader to contemplate the true cost of ambition in love. 

## Discussion launchpad
There are elements in Solomon's narrative that invite polarizing interpretations— the delicate balance of power dynamics in romance and the implications of ambition on personal relationships may stir debate. Is the pursuit of desire ultimately a distraction from professional fulfillment, or can they coexist harmoniously? What sacrifices are deemed acceptable in the name of love?

**Questions for the room:**  
- How do you interpret the balance between ambition and romance in the characters’ journeys?  
- Are the blurred lines of consent and power dynamics problematic or an authentic reflection of modern relationships?  
- What unresolved themes linger for you at the story's conclusion?  
- How does the fantastical element enhance or detract from the romance?  
- In what ways does this book challenge or conform to genre expectations?
