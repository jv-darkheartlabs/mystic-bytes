## Hook & thesis
Rachel Lynn Solomon's *Business or Pleasure* deftly navigates the labyrinth of desire and ambition, weaving a tale where the lines between personal and professional blur with intoxicating fervor. **Verdict:** This book is an audacious exploration of the complexities of passion, revealing that the most potent connections often thrive in the shadows of our ambitions.

## The contextual pivot
In the realm of fantasy-romance, Solomon's work stands apart by infusing contemporary nuances into a genre often dominated by more whimsical or escapist narratives. Unlike typical entries in this category, which may prioritize fantastical elements over genuine emotional depth, *Business or Pleasure* grounds its narrative in relatable stakes, appealing to an audience that seeks both enchantment and authenticity. This novel bridges niche tropes—such as the workplace romance and supernatural elements—while inviting general readers to reflect on their own aspirations and desires.

## Deep-dive critique
The structure of *Business or Pleasure* is a compelling blend of dual perspectives, allowing readers to intimately engage with the protagonists’ inner conflicts—each chapter oscillating between their individual aspirations and the electric tension simmering beneath their interactions. Solomon's pacing is expertly calibrated; moments of levity contrast with heavier themes, showcasing her adeptness at balancing the intricacies of human relationships. The dialogue crackles with wit and insight, revealing character motivations and desires without resorting to clichés. 

However, while the fantasy elements—like the magical realism woven through the narrative—present an intriguing backdrop, at times they risk overshadowing the core emotional journey. The world-building, while imaginative, occasionally feels underdeveloped compared to the rich character arcs, leading to moments where the plot's stakes diminish. Yet, the ending, filled with both resolution and the promise of new beginnings, feels earned and resonates with the themes of personal growth and connection.

## Discussion launchpad
The interplay of ambition and desire in *Business or Pleasure* raises provocative questions about the sacrifices we make in pursuit of our dreams, and how love can both illuminate and complicate our paths.
- How do ambition and romance coexist, and what does the novel suggest about their relationship?
- In portraying workplace dynamics infused with fantasy, does the novel reinforce or challenge traditional romantic tropes?
- What does the book imply about the nature of desire—can it coexist with personal ambition without conflict?

**Questions for the room:**
- How does Solomon’s use of dual perspectives enhance or detract from the central themes?
- Are the fantasy elements a necessary device, or do they distract from the authenticity of the romantic journey?
- What moments in the book most powerfully encapsulate the tension between personal ambition and romantic desire?
