## Hook & thesis
In *A Court of Hearts and Hunger*, Rebecca F. Kenney spins a web of twisted desire and perilous allegiance, where the very fabric of Wonderland is steeped in hunger and darkness. **Verdict:** This masterful retelling entwines themes of power and restraint, revealing that in a world governed by appetite, the fiercest hunger may be for connection itself.

## The contextual pivot
Kenney's work operates within the vibrant subgenre of fantasy-romance, drawing on the rich tapestry of Lewis Carroll's Wonderland while infusing it with a darker, more visceral palette. As a contemporary author, Kenney navigates the currents of romantasy, challenging both genre expectations and reader sensibilities. This book appeals to a niche yet growing audience that seeks more than whimsical escapism; it craves the intoxicating blend of peril and passion found in darker retellings. The intricate interplay of courtly intrigue and romantic tension ensures it resonates with fans of authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout, who have similarly bridged niche tropes to wider audiences.

## Deep-dive critique
Kenney's prose is a lush tapestry, rich with vivid imagery that immerses the reader in a world where desire is both intoxicating and dangerous. The pacing is deliberate, allowing the tension to simmer slowly, drawing readers deeper into the labyrinthine court politics and the protagonist's emotional struggles. The dual POV structure reveals the competing desires of both lovers, enhancing the stakes with every alternating chapter. However, while this choice deepens engagement, it occasionally disrupts the narrative flow, leading to moments where the suspense feels artificially prolonged.

Characterization stands out, particularly in the protagonist, whose internal conflict resonates with anyone who's navigated the treacherous waters of love and power. Dialogue crackles with wit and subtext, serving to heighten both romantic and political tension seamlessly. Yet, there are instances where secondary characters feel underdeveloped — they serve the plot but lack the depth to evoke genuine emotional investment, which can dilute the overall impact of pivotal scenes. The ending, however, is a triumph of thematic resonance; it poignantly encapsulates the central conflict and leaves the reader grappling with the bitter sweetness of sacrifice and longing.

## Discussion launchpad
In the realm of dark romance, certain elements could polarize readers: the blurred lines between love and obsession, the moral ambiguity of the characters, and the heavy reliance on courtly intrigue versus emotional depth. What remains unaddressed is the potential for deeper explorations of the consequences of power dynamics within relationships.  
**Questions for the room:**  
- How do you perceive the balance between power and desire in the relationship dynamics?  
- Does the darker tone enhance or inhibit the romantic elements for you?  
- Are there moments when the characters' actions felt justified, or did they cross a line?  
- How does the retelling aspect shape your reading experience compared to original tales?  
- What themes in this book resonate with contemporary societal issues regarding relationships and power?
