## Hook & thesis
*Compulsion* by Zoey Ellis navigates the primal waters of desire and dominance within the omegaverse, crafting a narrative that entwines ferocity with delicate beauty. **Verdict:** This novella masterfully embodies the tension between instinct and will, plunging readers into a world where every heartbeat echoes with the threat of submission.

## The contextual pivot
As a standalone entry in the *Myth of Omega* series, *Compulsion* occupies a provocative niche within the dark romance genre, particularly the subgenre of omegaverse. Ellis skillfully harnesses tropes of power dynamics, exploring the complexities of identity and belonging. Readers familiar with this genre will recognize its rich tapestry woven with threads of hierarchy and desire; however, Ellis's work also invites broader audiences who may be drawn to the themes of transformation and the primal aspects of romantic relationships. This narrative bridges niche tropes with a broader exploration of human connection, inviting those unacquainted with omegaverse dynamics into an intoxicating experience.

## Deep-dive critique
Ellis employs a tightly woven structure that enhances the novella's pacing, allowing tension to build steadily until it reaches a boiling point. The third-person limited POV allows readers to deeply inhabit the psyche of the main character, who grapples with the intoxicating pull of their instincts against the backdrop of societal expectations. This duality heightens the emotional stakes, as the character's struggle becomes a reflection of the broader theme of autonomy versus compulsion.

The prose itself is lush and evocative, laden with botanical imagery that mirrors the themes of growth and entrapment. Sentences bloom with rich descriptions, forging an intimate connection between the reader and the characters' desires. Dialogue sparkles with subtext, revealing the characters' inner conflicts and power plays, effectively drawing the reader further into the intricate web of their relationships.

However, while the thematic explorations are compelling, the brevity of the novella occasionally leaves certain character arcs feeling truncated. Some readers may find the resolution unsatisfyingly abrupt, as the emotional payoff seems to rush past the careful buildup. Yet, this may also serve to emphasize the chaotic essence of desire itself — a whirlwind that can leave one breathless, longing, and yearning for more.

## Discussion launchpad
*Compulsion* straddles the line between darkly romantic and unsettling, provoking questions about the nature of consent and desire in relationships that are governed by instinct. The tension between beauty and ferocity invites a valuable exploration of how these dynamics play out in personal connections.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the novella's structure influence your perception of the characters' journeys?  
- In what ways do you believe Ellis navigates the complexities of consent within the omegaverse framework?  
- Do you think the ending is earned, or does it leave too much unresolved?  
- How does the botanical imagery function as a metaphor for the characters' struggles?  
- What are the implications of desire when instinct is at odds with societal expectations?
