## Hook & thesis
In Carissa Broadbent's *Children of Fallen Gods*, the author plunges into a tempest of desire and moral ambiguity, crafting a narrative that intertwines divine machinations with the raw pulse of human emotion. **Verdict:** This book is an intoxicating exploration of the conflict between duty and desire, where the stakes are life and love, and to choose one may cost the other.

## The contextual pivot
Within the expansive realm of fantasy-romance, Broadbent's work occupies a unique niche, bridging the darker, more intricate elements of gothic romance with the epic scope of high fantasy. Her narrative continues the *War of Lost Hearts* series, a follow-up that deepens character complexity while weaving a rich tapestry of magic and conflict. This volume particularly appeals to readers who revel in morally grey characters and tumultuous relationships, reminiscent of authors like Sarah J. Maas and Jennifer L. Armentrout, yet it possesses a distinctive edge that may attract a broader audience beyond established fans of the genre.

## Deep-dive critique
Broadbent's craftsmanship shines through her deft structure, where alternating perspectives offer insight into the characters' inner turmoil and desires. The pacing oscillates effectively between moments of frenetic action and contemplative introspection, creating a rhythm that mirrors the turbulent emotions at play. This technique immerses readers in the duality of longing and betrayal, as protagonists grapple with their fateful ties to the divine and each other.

The prose is often lyrical, evoking vivid imagery that serves the thematic core of choice and sacrifice. For instance, Broadbent’s imagery around the protagonists' encounters with the gods encapsulates a sense of both wonder and dread, perfectly embodying the gothic romance aesthetic. Dialogue is sharp and evocative, allowing characters to express their desires and conflicts without excessive exposition, maintaining an air of subtle tension that envelops the narrative.

However, while the ending attempts to deliver a satisfying resolution, it risks feeling abrupt against the richly layered build-up. Some character arcs may resonate deeply while others leave lingering questions, potentially frustrating readers who seek closure. Yet, this ambiguity can also serve as a deliberate choice, reflecting the unresolved nature of desire and consequence.

## Discussion launchpad
Broadbent's work ignites potential debates on the moral implications of desire and the consequences of divine intervention. The exploration of characters trapped between their loyalties and personal desires lays fertile ground for rich discussion, particularly regarding the ethics of choice in a world governed by gods.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the duality of duty and desire manifest in the characters’ choices?  
- In what ways do the protagonists' relationships challenge traditional fantasy tropes?  
- Does the abruptness of the ending enhance or detract from the story's overall impact?  
- How does Broadbent's world-building contribute to the theme of emotional entrapment?  
- What does the narrative suggest about the nature of power and its influence on personal relationships?
