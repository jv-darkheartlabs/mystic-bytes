## Hook & thesis
In *A Homerun Proposal*, Maren Moore crafts a tantalizing enemies-to-lovers narrative that digs into the complexities of rivalry and vulnerability. **Verdict:** This book deftly intertwines the heat of competition with the rawness of desire, making it a compelling exploration of love on the field and off.

## The contextual pivot
Situated in the contemporary dark romance landscape, *A Homerun Proposal* melds the thrill of sports with the charged dynamics of personal animosity. Moore's exploration of the enemies-to-lovers trope leans into the depths of human emotion, echoing themes found in works by authors like Tessa Bailey and Sarah J. Maas, who similarly navigate the terrain where passion and animosity collide. The book deftly bridges niche sports themes with broader romantic narratives, appealing to both die-hard sports enthusiasts and those drawn to character-driven love stories. Its accessibility invites a diverse readership, proving that even the most unlikely partnerships can yield profound connections.

## Deep-dive critique
Maren Moore’s narrative structure effectively utilizes a dual POV, allowing readers to experience the conflicting motivations of both protagonists. This choice amplifies the tension, as we oscillate between their perspectives, heightening the stakes of their rivalry. The pacing is commendable, with a slow burn that gradually ignites, drawing readers into the intensity of their encounters. Dialogue sparkles with wit and sarcasm, capturing the essence of their contentious relationship while subtly underlining the chemistry that simmers beneath the surface.

However, the prose occasionally falters with moments of cliché that could have been avoided, particularly in the realm of sports metaphors. These moments risk undermining the otherwise sharp character development. Moore excels in world-building within the sports arena, vividly depicting the electric atmosphere of games and the emotional weight of public scrutiny, which adds layers to the protagonists' connection. The climax, culminating in a pivotal proposal scene, is both unexpected and earned, encapsulating their journey from animosity to affection in a way that feels organic rather than forced.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel’s blend of sports and romance raises intriguing questions about the nature of competition and intimacy. Readers may find themselves debating the effectiveness of the enemies-to-lovers trope in this context and how it reflects real-life dynamics in competitive environments. The tension between personal ambition and romantic involvement also presents a rich vein for discussion. 

**Questions for the room:**
- How does the enemies-to-lovers trope function differently in a sports setting compared to more traditional environments?
- Do you feel the tension between rivalry and romance was effectively portrayed, or did it lean too heavily into cliché?
- How does the dual POV enhance or detract from the emotional stakes of the story?
- What are the implications of public versus private personas in shaping the characters' relationship?
- How does Moore's portrayal of competition influence your understanding of desire and vulnerability in the context of love?
