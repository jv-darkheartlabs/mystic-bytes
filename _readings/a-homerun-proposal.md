## Hook & thesis
In "A Homerun Proposal," Maren Moore masterfully entwines the fierce rivalry of professional sports with the simmering tension of enemies-to-lovers romance. **Verdict:** This novel is a daring exploration of ambition and vulnerability, revealing how deep-seated grudges can morph into intoxicating desire.

## The contextual pivot
Set against the backdrop of competitive athletics, this book occupies a curious niche in the enemies-to-lovers genre, where characters clash not merely over personal stakes but also professional legacies. Moore's work aligns with authors like Talia Hibbert and Christina Lauren, who deftly blend romantic tension with humor and character evolution. However, Moore's approach leans darker, delving into the psychological complexities of rivalry and aspiration, making it accessible to both devoted fans of the trope and those seeking a more nuanced exploration of romance's darker edges. The invisible audience here finds resonance in the emotional stakes of sports, connecting the competitive spirit with the passion that simmers beneath the surface.

## Deep-dive critique
Moore's narrative structure propels the reader through a deftly crafted timeline, oscillating between moments of high-stakes competition and private revelations. The pacing is particularly effective; chapter lengths vary, mirroring the tension of a game—some quick, adrenaline-fueled bursts, while others linger on the aftermath of conflict, allowing characters to breathe and evolve. The use of dual perspectives enriches the narrative, inviting readers into the minds of both protagonists, where ambition clashes with desire.

Character development stands out as a high point; Moore excels in crafting flawed yet relatable figures whose rivalry is deeply rooted in their respective aspirations. The dialogue crackles with wit and tension, often layered with unspoken feelings. For instance, a seemingly innocuous exchange during a training session belies a deeper longing, showcasing Moore's ability to convey heat through subtext rather than explicit detail. 

However, the book is not without its mechanical flaws. While the tension between the leads is palpable, certain plot points lean heavily into cliché, risking predictability in areas where the stakes could have been raised further. The resolution, though satisfying on a surface level, may not feel fully earned to some readers, leaving a lingering question of whether the characters genuinely overcame their pasts or simply capitulated to desire. 

## Discussion launchpad
This text invites a lively examination of the intricacies of rivalry and romance, weaving together themes of ambition, vulnerability, and the thin line between love and hate. 

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does Moore’s portrayal of competition influence the romantic arc?  
- In what ways does the setting enhance or detract from the central conflict?  
- Are the character flaws presented as redeemable, or do they pose insurmountable barriers to true intimacy?  
- How does the dual perspective shape our understanding of the conflict?  
- What are the implications of the ending for our perception of growth in the characters?
