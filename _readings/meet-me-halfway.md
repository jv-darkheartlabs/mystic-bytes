## Hook & thesis
In *Meet Me Halfway*, Lilian T. James deftly weaves a tale where desire grapples with societal constraints, laying bare the complexities of forbidden love in a manner that is both tantalizing and harrowing. **Verdict:** This novel captures the essence of yearning, revealing that true connection often resides in the shadows of convention.

## The contextual pivot
Placing *Meet Me Halfway* within the forbidden love genre, it stands alongside works like *The Forbidden* by Kira Jane Buxton and *A Court of Mist and Fury* by Sarah J. Maas. Here, James navigates the tension between societal expectation and personal desire, a hallmark of such narratives. This book speaks to a diverse audience, particularly those who appreciate the delicate dance of second chances and the intricacies of emotional entanglements, bridging niche tropes with broader themes of love lost and reclaimed.

## Deep-dive critique
James's structure is compelling, employing a dual POV that allows readers to intimately experience the internal struggles of both protagonists. The shifting timelines enhance the sense of nostalgia, underscoring the weight of past decisions on present desires. However, this choice also introduces moments where pacing falters, particularly in the middle sections, where the emotional buildup may feel overly drawn out, detracting from the urgency of the unfolding romance.

The prose is lush, dripping with evocative imagery, effectively crafting a world where each encounter is charged with unspoken tension. Dialogue shines, revealing character depth and history without overt exposition. However, some exchanges can border on melodramatic, threatening the otherwise polished veneer of the narrative. 

The thematic exploration of desire versus duty resonates powerfully; James deftly intertwines personal stakes with societal expectations, forcing characters to confront their deepest fears and desires. The ending is particularly poignant, offering both resolution and ambiguity, leaving readers to ponder the cost of love.

## Discussion launchpad
The novel's exploration of forbidden love ignites polarizing questions about societal norms and personal happiness—what sacrifices are too great for love? The unresolved tensions around commitment and familial expectations linger, inviting debate on the nature of desire itself.

**Questions for the room:**  
- How does the dual POV affect your connection to the characters?  
- In what ways does James challenge or reinforce societal norms through her characters' choices?  
- Is the balance of past and present timelines effective, or does it detract from the narrative’s momentum?  
- What are the implications of the ending—does it feel earned or contrived?  
- How does the depiction of forbidden love resonate in today’s societal climate?
