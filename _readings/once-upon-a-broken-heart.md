## Hook & thesis
In *Once Upon a Broken Heart*, Stephanie Garber weaves a tale steeped in enchantment and betrayal, exploring the intricate dance between desire and consequence. **Verdict:** Garber captures the essence of dark romanticism by illustrating that every wish can yield heartbreak, making sacrifice the bittersweet currency of love.

## The contextual pivot
Positioned within the fantasy-romance genre, Garber's latest work revisits themes of enchanted bargains and the perils of desire, echoing the allure of authors like Sarah J. Maas and the whimsical darkness found in Holly Black's narratives. This novel caters to a niche audience that revels in fairy-tale retellings laced with emotional depth, yet it possesses the potential to ensnare general readers with its approachable prose and captivating romance. The book serves as a bridge, drawing in those who may not typically engage with darker tales, offering a gateway to the complexities of desire and consequence.

## Deep-dive critique
Garber's structural choices play a pivotal role in shaping the narrative's emotional core. The first-person perspective effectively immerses readers in Evangeline's turbulent emotions, allowing her heartbreak and yearning to resonate deeply. The pacing, however, occasionally falters; moments of anticipation can stretch too thin, leading to a sense of drag that undermines the urgency of the unfolding stakes. 

On the craft front, Garber's prose shines, imbued with a lyrical quality that elevates mundane moments into something magical. Dialogues carry a playful tension, rife with subtext that hints at unspoken desires. However, certain character arcs—most notably supporting roles—lack the depth and nuance necessary to fully embody the stakes of the central conflict. The world-building, while vividly imagined, sometimes leans on familiar tropes, risking predictability in a realm that thrives on innovation. The climax, while dramatic, feels somewhat rushed, leaving readers yearning for a more fleshed-out resolution that aptly reflects the journey of sacrifice undertaken by Evangeline. 

## Discussion launchpad
Garber’s work invites a provocative exploration of love's complexities as we question the costs of desire and the nature of sacrifice. While the romantic tension captivates, there are gaps in character development that may polarize readers.

**Questions for the room:**  
- Does the portrayal of sacrifice in Evangeline's journey resonate as authentic or contrived?  
- How do you perceive the balance of darkness and whimsy in Garber's storytelling?  
- Are the stakes of Evangeline's choices effectively conveyed, or do they diminish as the plot unfolds?  
- In what ways does this novel compare to traditional fairy tales, and does it successfully subvert those expectations?  
- Should fantasy-romance embrace darker themes more frequently, or does this risk alienating a broader audience?
